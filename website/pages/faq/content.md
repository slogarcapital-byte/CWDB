## Questions & Answers

### 1. How does Central Wisconsin Deck Builders work?

We make it simple. You fill out a quick form telling us about your deck project — size, materials, timeline, budget. We review your request and match you with a vetted, licensed contractor in your area. The contractor reaches out to discuss your project and provide a detailed quote. The whole process is free for homeowners, and there's never any obligation to move forward.

---

### 2. Is the quote really free?

Yes — 100% free. There's no charge to submit a request, no hidden fees, and no cost if you decide not to move forward. We believe you should be able to get pricing information without opening your wallet.

---

### 3. How quickly will I hear back?

Most homeowners hear from a contractor within 48 hours. Response times depend on the contractor's schedule, but we prioritize fast turnaround because we know waiting is frustrating.

---

### 4. How do you choose which contractors are in your network?

Every contractor goes through a vetting process before they're approved. We verify:

- Active Wisconsin contractor license
- General liability insurance
- Workers' compensation coverage
- Track record of residential deck projects in Central Wisconsin
- Customer references

If a contractor doesn't meet our standards, they don't join the network. We also monitor credentials over time to make sure nothing lapses.

---

### 5. What areas do you serve?

We currently serve homeowners in:

- [[Wausau]] (including Rib Mountain)
- [[Schofield]]
- [[Weston]]
- [[Mosinee]]
- [[Merrill]]

We're expanding into additional Central Wisconsin communities soon. If you're nearby, go ahead and [submit a request](/get-a-quote) — we'll do our best to connect you with a builder.

---

### 6. What types of deck projects do you handle?

Our contractors handle a wide range of residential deck projects, including:

- New deck construction (ground level and elevated)
- Multi-level and wraparound decks
- Composite and PVC decking
- Pressure-treated wood decks
- Cedar and hardwood decks
- Deck repairs and board replacement
- Deck resurfacing and refinishing
- Screened porches and covered deck additions
- Pergolas and shade structures
- Railing and stair upgrades

If you're not sure whether your project qualifies, submit a request and we'll let you know.

---

### 7. How much does a deck cost in Central Wisconsin?

Deck costs vary depending on size, materials, design, and site conditions. Here are rough ranges for the [[Wausau]] area:

- **Basic pressure-treated deck (12x16):** $4,000–$8,000
- **Composite deck (12x16):** $8,000–$15,000
- **Multi-level or custom design:** $15,000–$30,000+
- **Deck repair or resurfacing:** $1,500–$5,000

These are ballpark figures. The best way to get an accurate number is to [request a free quote](/get-a-quote) for your specific project.

---

### 8. Do I need a permit to build a deck in Wisconsin?

In most cases, yes. Wisconsin municipalities generally require a building permit for new deck construction and significant structural modifications. The specific requirements depend on your local building department — [[Wausau]], [[Weston]], [[Schofield]], [[Mosinee]], and [[Merrill]] each have their own permitting process.

The good news: your contractor typically handles the permit application as part of the project. When you get your quote, ask your builder about permit requirements and costs for your area.

---

### 9. What decking materials are available?

The most common materials used by our contractors include:

- **Pressure-treated lumber** — The most affordable option. Durable and widely available. Requires periodic staining or sealing.
- **Composite decking (e.g., Trex, TimberTech)** — Low-maintenance, long-lasting, and available in many colors and textures. Higher upfront cost but less maintenance over time.
- **PVC decking** — Fully synthetic, extremely low maintenance, and resistant to moisture and staining.
- **Cedar** — Naturally rot-resistant with a classic look. Requires maintenance but ages beautifully.
- **Hardwoods (e.g., ipe)** — Premium option with exceptional durability. Highest cost but longest lifespan.

Your contractor can recommend the best material for your budget, climate, and design preferences.

---

### 10. What if I'm just in the planning phase and not ready to build yet?

That's completely fine. Many homeowners use our service to get a ballpark quote before making any decisions. There's no obligation to hire anyone, and we'll never pressure you. A quote gives you real pricing to factor into your planning — whether you're building this summer or next year.

---

### 11. Can I choose which contractor I work with?

We match you with the contractor in our network who's the best fit for your project based on location, availability, and specialization. As our network grows, you may have the option to receive quotes from multiple builders and choose the one you prefer.

---

### 12. Is my information shared with anyone?

Your contact and project details are shared only with the vetted contractor(s) matched to your project. We do not sell your information to marketing lists, spam databases, or unrelated third parties. For full details, see our [Privacy Policy](/privacy).
