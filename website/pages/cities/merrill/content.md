## Frequently Asked Questions — Deck Building in Merrill

### How much does a new deck cost in Merrill, WI?

Deck projects in Merrill generally run between $5,500 and $18,000. A standard 12x16 pressure-treated wood deck on a single-level home typically costs $5,500 to $10,000 — the most common project type in the Merrill area. Composite builds and larger footprints push costs higher, usually in the $12,000 to $18,000 range. Merrill's relatively flat residential lots and straightforward soil conditions often keep foundation costs lower than in hillier parts of the region. Request a free quote for exact pricing on your property.

### Do I need a building permit for a deck in Merrill?

Yes. The City of Merrill requires building permits for new deck construction and for structural modifications to existing decks. Permits cover footing depth, railing height, structural capacity, and setback requirements. If your property is outside city limits in the Town of Merrill or elsewhere in Lincoln County, county-level permitting may apply instead. Your contractor will know which jurisdiction covers your address and can handle the permit paperwork.

### Is it worth replacing my deck or should I just repair it?

It depends on the structural condition. If the support posts and joists are solid but the deck boards and railing are worn, a surface-level repair or board replacement can extend the life of the deck for several more years at lower cost. If the posts are rotting at the base, the ledger board is pulling away from the house, or the footings are shifting, a full replacement is the safer and more cost-effective path. Your contractor will assess the structure during the quote process and give you an honest recommendation.

### What is the most cost-effective deck material for Merrill homeowners?

Pressure-treated lumber remains the most affordable option and is the most common choice in Merrill. It costs roughly 40 to 50 percent less than composite upfront and performs well for 15 to 20 years with regular staining and sealing every two to three years. For homeowners who want to avoid ongoing maintenance, composite decking costs more initially but eliminates annual upkeep. Your contractor can price both options so you can compare the real numbers.

Our contractor network covers Merrill and the surrounding Lincoln County area, including properties in the Town of Merrill, Town of Pine River, and along the Highway 51 corridor north toward Tomahawk. If you are within a reasonable drive of Merrill, submit a quote request and we will confirm coverage for your address.
