# Impeccable Design Context — CWDB

This file satisfies the `/impeccable` skill's teach-mode precondition. It establishes design context so `/impeccable craft` and the companion refinement skills (`/critique`, `/polish`, `/distill`, `/bolder`, `/quieter`, `/colorize`, `/typeset`, `/layout`, `/delight`, `/audit`, `/harden`) can run without a one-off teach pass.

**Scope:** This Design Context is written for **advertising creatives** (Meta, Google Display, Nextdoor, TikTok). Ads interrupt; the website reassures. These are different craft contexts. If website design work later needs its own context, append a separate `## Design Context (Website)` section — do not overwrite this one.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21

## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are.** Central Wisconsin homeowners aged 35–60 with a deck project in mind. Primary cities: Wausau, Schofield, Weston, Mosinee, Merrill. Typical profile: two-income household, one or two kids, owns the house, has thought about the deck for 1–3 years, doesn't know who to call, has been burned by at least one contractor in their life (or knows someone who was).

**Persona shorthand:** the "Stressed Delegator" (from `/business-context/brand-discovery/brand-voice-positioning.md`). They want it handled — they do not want a project to manage.

**Context when they see the creative.** Scrolling a feed on a phone at 9pm after kids are in bed. Mentally checked out. Not shopping. Not in "make a decision" mode. The creative has roughly **0.8 seconds** to earn a thumb-stop.

**The job to be done.** Get a trustworthy deck quote fast, without calling six strangers and comparing confusing proposals.

---

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Blue-collar · Plainspoken · Skilled.

**NOT these words:** modern, elegant, premium, luxury, innovative, cutting-edge, revolutionary. These are dead categories for this audience — Central Wisconsin homeowners distrust anyone who talks like that.

**Reference aesthetic:** Milwaukee Tool packaging. Caterpillar equipment decals. A carpenter's union hall. A Menards Sunday circular, but well-designed. Things that look like they were made by people who make things.

**Anti-references (do not resemble these):**
- Thumbtack / HomeAdvisor / Angi feed ads — blue gradients, stock handshake photos, generic script typography, "Get matched in minutes!" energy
- SaaS landing-page aesthetics — purple gradients, cyan accents, floating cards with soft shadows
- Pinterest-ready "dream deck" moodboards — soft-focus, pastel, aspirational
- Any ad that looks like it was made in Canva from a template

**Emotional goals (what the creative should evoke):**
- Relief ("finally, someone just does it")
- Recognition ("that looks like a deck in my neighborhood, not a magazine")
- Trust ("these are real people, not a lead-gen middleman")
- Mild urgency ("summer is short, and I've been putting this off")

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** Mostly light (off-white and white backgrounds) with occasional dark (Timber Slate) for hero imagery contrast. Dark mode is not a design toggle here; it's a compositional choice per-creative based on photo exposure.

**Composition:** Photo-forward, zero-decoration. The hero photo occupies **60–70%** of the frame. Type supports the photo, never the reverse. Stock photography never appears — we use the 5 real Wisconsin deck photos in `/branding/logos/web/`:
- `hero-wausau.webp`
- `hero-schofield.webp`
- `hero-weston.webp`
- `hero-mosinee.webp`
- `hero-merrill.webp`

**Typography (locked to site):**
- Display: **Staatliches** (Google Fonts, 400 weight only). Uppercase, narrow, civic-industrial. Letter-spacing 0.5px. Used for headlines.
- Body: **Public Sans** (Google Fonts, weights 400 / 500 / 600 / 700). Used for sub-copy, trust rows, CTA labels.
- Both fonts must be `<link>`-loaded from Google Fonts in every creative HTML file — Playwright's Chromium will not have them otherwise.

**Color palette (locked to site, hex primary, OKLCH secondary for gradient-free tinting):**
| Role | Hex | OKLCH (approx) | Usage in ads |
|------|-----|----------------|--------------|
| Crafted Orange | `#e54c00` | `oklch(0.59 0.20 37)` | CTA background. One appearance per creative. Never as secondary accent. |
| Timber Slate | `#323434` | `oklch(0.28 0.003 180)` | Primary text on light; background on dark-hero compositions |
| Builders Grey | `#646760` | `oklch(0.45 0.006 120)` | Trust-row text, fine print, dividers |
| Off-White | `#f8f8f6` | `oklch(0.98 0.003 100)` | Primary light background |
| White | `#ffffff` | `oklch(1.00 0 0)` | Secondary light surface |
| Wisconsin Sky | `#83b2cf` | `oklch(0.74 0.06 235)` | **Not used in ads.** Sky is a website signal-accent color; in paid creatives it competes with orange for attention. Leave it out. |

**Motion:** Static only for now. No animated creatives until video pipeline exists.

**Absolute bans (from `/impeccable`, enforced without exception in ads):**
1. **No side-stripe borders** — `border-left` or `border-right` with width > 1px. Banned regardless of color or opacity.
2. **No gradient text** — `background-clip: text` combined with any gradient. Solid colors only.
3. **No generic reflex fonts** — Inter, Roboto, Arial, Open Sans, DM Sans, Space Grotesk, Fraunces, Plus Jakarta Sans, Playfair Display, IBM Plex, Outfit, Newsreader, Lora, Crimson, Instrument, Syne, Cormorant. (If a creative ever needs a third font beyond Staatliches/Public Sans, it must not be on the reflex list.)
4. **No AI cyan-to-purple gradient palettes, no glow-on-dark neon accents, no glassmorphism decorative blur.**
5. **No stock photography.** Ever.
6. **No pure `#000` or pure `#ffffff` text** — tint toward the brand hue (already built into Timber Slate and Off-White above).

---

### Design Principles

Six rules that govern every creative. These override personal taste and AI reflex.

1. **One thing per frame.** One angle, one benefit, one CTA. No feature lists, no sub-bullets, no "also + also." If a creative needs two angles, make it two creatives.

2. **Photo is the hero.** Type supports the photo, never reversed. If the photo isn't doing 60%+ of the communication, the creative is wrong.

3. **Orange lies once.** Crafted Orange (`#e54c00`) appears in exactly **one** place per creative — almost always the CTA pill. Do not tint section backgrounds, icon wrappers, dividers, or underlines with orange. Orange means "do this thing." Everywhere else is photo, slate, off-white, and grey.

4. **Sound like a neighbor, not a marketer.** Headline test: could this have come out of the mouth of a homeowner at a Friday-night bonfire? "Fast quotes from builders you'd actually hire" passes. "Transform your outdoor living space today" fails. For Nextdoor specifically, the test gets stricter — copy must read as conversational post, not ad-copy.

5. **Unmistakable as CWDB.** Someone who saw our website yesterday must be able to recognize this creative as ours **without reading the logo**. Achieved via the fixed type lockup (Staatliches headline over Public Sans sub), the photo treatment (Wisconsin decks, natural light, no filters), and the single-hit-of-orange rule.

6. **Break an impeccable ban only deliberately, or not at all.** The absolute bans above are unconditional. If a creative requires breaking one, it needs a written justification in its `-notes.md` sidecar explaining the aesthetic reason. Breaking a ban "because it looked better" is not a reason — that's the trained reflex talking.

## When to re-run `/impeccable teach`

This context covers residential-homeowner paid creatives in Central Wisconsin. Re-run teach (scoped to the new context) if any of these change:

- A new audience enters scope (e.g. commercial deck repair, multifamily, property managers)
- A new channel enters scope that breaks the static-poster assumption (video-first TikTok, podcast sponsorship audio, print)
- A new geography with materially different cultural context enters scope (Minneapolis metro has different homeowner vernacular than Central Wisconsin)
- The brand voice is retuned (re-read `brand-voice-positioning.md` — if the Hammer/Handshake rule changes, teach must re-run)
