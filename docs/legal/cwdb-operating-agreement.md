## Plain-English Summary (Read First)

This Operating Agreement is the rulebook for how Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC (the "Company") is owned and run. Jim Slogar is the only owner (the sole "Member") and runs the Company himself (it is "member-managed").

The most important thing this document does for the current plan: it **expressly authorizes two business lines under one LLC** at the same time:

1. **Lead generation:** generating homeowner deck-project leads and selling them to contractors.
2. **Construction:** building, repairing, replacing, resurfacing, staining, refinishing, and improving residential decks directly.

Because both lines live in one LLC instead of two separate companies, the legal protection that a second company would have given you has to be recreated here. That protection is your **liability shield**: the rule that the Company's debts and lawsuits stay with the Company and do not reach Jim personally. Under Wisconsin law (Wis. Stat. s. 183.0304), that shield exists, but courts can disregard it if the owner treats the LLC as an alter ego, commingles money, or leaves the business badly underinsured.

So this Agreement builds in three covenants that you must actually follow to keep the shield strong:

- **Separation covenant:** keep Company money in Company accounts, never mix it with personal money.
- **Insurance covenant:** keep commercial general liability insurance in force, and keep any legally required workers' compensation in force while construction operations are active.
- **Separate-books covenant:** track the lead-gen line and the construction line as two distinct profit centers, with separate bookkeeping.

If you follow these covenants, the single-LLC structure is defensible. If you ignore them, you give a future plaintiff the argument that the LLC should be pierced. This is the governance substitute for not forming a second entity. Treat it as load-bearing, not boilerplate. The risk analysis is rated **HIGH** and is detailed in `docs/legal/construction-setup/entity-structure-and-spinout-memo.md`.

## ARTICLE 1. FORMATION

1.1. The Company was organized as a limited liability company under the Wisconsin Uniform Limited Liability Company Law, Wis. Stat. ch. 183, by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Wisconsin Department of Financial Institutions, effective April 6, 2026, Entity ID C138564.

1.2. This Operating Agreement is adopted by the sole Member pursuant to Wis. Stat. s. 183.0105 and governs the affairs of the Company and the conduct of its business. To the extent this Agreement is silent, the default rules of Wis. Stat. ch. 183 apply.

1.3. The existence of the Company is perpetual unless dissolved as provided in Article 11 or by law.

## ARTICLE 2. NAME, OFFICE, AND REGISTERED AGENT

2.1. The name of the Company is Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC.

2.2. The principal office and place of business is 906 N 16th Ave, Wausau, Wisconsin 54401, or such other place as the Member may designate.

2.3. The registered agent and registered office on file with the State of Wisconsin is James Slogar at the address in Section 2.2, consistent with Wis. Stat. s. 183.0115. The Member shall keep this information current.

## ARTICLE 3. PURPOSE AND AUTHORIZED BUSINESS

3.1. The Company is organized to engage in any lawful activity for which a limited liability company may be organized under Wis. Stat. ch. 183.

3.2. Without limiting Section 3.1, the Company is expressly authorized to conduct, simultaneously and as distinct business lines, both of the following:

(a) **Lead Generation Line.** Generating, qualifying, and selling homeowner deck-project leads and related marketing services to contractors and other purchasers, including the operation of websites, advertising campaigns, and lead-routing systems.

(b) **Construction Line.** Performing residential deck construction, building, repair, replacement, resurfacing, staining, refinishing, and improvement, and any related home-improvement services, in the State of Wisconsin, subject to the Company holding all credentials required by Wis. Stat. ch. 101 and Wis. Admin. Code ch. SPS 305, including the Dwelling Contractor Certification, and to compliance with the Wisconsin home improvement practices rules at Wis. Admin. Code ch. ATCP 110.

3.3. The Member intends that both lines operate side by side under this single Company. The authorization of the Construction Line does not limit, replace, or wind down the Lead Generation Line.

3.4. The Company may engage subcontractors, including converting existing contractor relationships into subcontractor relationships, to perform Construction Line work, subject to written subcontractor agreements and proof of the subcontractor's own insurance and credentials.

## ARTICLE 4. MEMBER AND CAPITAL

4.1. **Sole Member.** James "Jim" Slogar is the sole Member of the Company and owns one hundred percent (100%) of the membership interest.

4.2. **Capital Contributions.** The Member has contributed, and may from time to time contribute, cash or property to the Company. The Member is not required to make additional capital contributions and has no personal liability for the debts of the Company except as provided by law.

4.3. **Capital Account.** A capital account shall be maintained for the Member in accordance with sound accounting practice.

## ARTICLE 5. DISTRIBUTIONS AND ALLOCATIONS

5.1. As a single-member LLC that is by default disregarded for federal income tax purposes, all income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit of the Company are allocated to the Member.

5.2. Distributions of cash or property may be made to the Member at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, subject to the limitations on distributions in Wis. Stat. s. 183.0405 (no distribution that would render the Company unable to pay its debts as they come due or leave it with assets less than liabilities).

5.3. If the Company makes an S-Corporation election, distributions and the Member's reasonable compensation shall be handled consistent with that election and applicable tax law. See `docs/legal/construction-setup/tax-treatment-memo.md`.

## ARTICLE 6. MANAGEMENT

6.1. **Member-Managed.** The Company is managed by its Member pursuant to Wis. Stat. s. 183.0407. The Member, acting as Manager, has full and exclusive authority to manage and control the business and affairs of the Company.

6.2. **Authority.** The Member/Manager may, without limitation: enter into and perform contracts (including homeowner construction contracts and subcontractor agreements); hire and supervise subcontractors and employees; open and operate bank accounts; bind and maintain insurance; apply for and hold licenses and credentials; borrow money; and execute any instrument on behalf of the Company.

6.3. **Standard of Conduct.** The Member/Manager shall discharge duties consistent with Wis. Stat. s. 183.0409, in good faith and in a manner the Member reasonably believes to be in the best interests of the Company.

## ARTICLE 7. LIABILITY SHIELD AND CONDITIONS FOR MAINTAINING IT

7.1. **Limited Liability.** Pursuant to Wis. Stat. s. 183.0304, the debts, obligations, and liabilities of the Company, whether arising in contract, tort, or otherwise, are solely the debts, obligations, and liabilities of the Company. The Member is not personally liable for them solely by reason of being the Member or acting as Manager.

7.2. **Acknowledged Risk of a Single Entity.** The Member acknowledges that operating the Construction Line and the Lead Generation Line within one LLC means that a claim arising from construction activity (for example, personal injury, structural failure, or property damage) can be asserted against all assets of the Company, including assets generated by the Lead Generation Line. The Member further acknowledges that a serious uninsured or underinsured judgment, or conduct that allows a court to disregard the LLC, could expose the Member personally. This risk is rated **HIGH** and is the reason for the covenants in this Article and in Articles 8 and 9.

7.3. **Conditions for Maintaining the Shield.** To preserve the limited-liability protection and reduce the risk that a court pierces the LLC veil, the Member covenants to:

(a) **No commingling.** Keep all Company funds in Company bank accounts separate from the Member's personal funds, and pay personal expenses from personal accounts only.

(b) **Adequate capitalization and insurance.** Maintain insurance as required by Article 8 and avoid leaving the Company so thinly capitalized as to be unable to meet reasonably foreseeable liabilities.

(c) **Formalities.** Sign contracts in the name of the Company, use the Company name and credential numbers in dealings with the public, and keep the records required by Article 9.

(d) **Honest dealing.** Not use the Company to commit fraud or to evade an obligation of the Member.

7.4. The Member acknowledges that failure to honor Section 7.3 weakens the liability shield and may permit a creditor to reach the Member personally.

## ARTICLE 8. INSURANCE COVENANT

8.1. **Commercial General Liability.** While the Company conducts business, the Member shall cause the Company to maintain commercial general liability (CGL) insurance in commercially reasonable amounts appropriate to residential deck construction. Target coverages and limits are described in `docs/legal/construction-setup/insurance-spec-sheet.md`; the Member shall verify adequacy with a licensed insurance broker.

8.2. **Workers' Compensation.** While the Construction Line is active and the Company has employees or otherwise meets the statutory threshold, the Member shall cause the Company to maintain workers' compensation insurance as required by Wis. Stat. ch. 102. The Member shall verify the applicable threshold with the Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development or a licensed Wisconsin attorney, because the requirement turns on the number of employees and payroll.

8.3. **Subcontractor Insurance.** Before any subcontractor performs Construction Line work, the Member shall obtain a current certificate of insurance from that subcontractor naming the Company as an additional insured, consistent with the subcontractor agreement at `docs/legal/templates/subcontractor-agreement-template.md`.

8.4. The insurance covenant is a material condition of maintaining the liability shield under Article 7. Allowing required coverage to lapse during active operations is a breach of this Agreement by the Member against the interests of the Company.

## ARTICLE 9. BOOKS, RECORDS, AND SEPARATE PROFIT TRACKING

9.1. The Company shall keep accurate books and records at its principal office, consistent with Wis. Stat. s. 183.0410.

9.2. **Separate Bookkeeping by Business Line (Required).** The Member shall maintain bookkeeping that separately tracks the revenue, direct costs, and profit of (a) the Lead Generation Line and (b) the Construction Line, as two distinct profit centers. This separation is required for three reasons: it supports the liability and alter-ego analysis under Article 7, it supports correct sales/use tax treatment of construction work under Wis. Stat. ch. 77 and Wis. Admin. Code ch. Tax 11, and it gives the Member and any CPA a clean basis for the S-Corp reasonable-compensation analysis.

9.3. The Member may use a single Company operating bank account but shall use accounting classes, tags, or subaccounts sufficient to produce a separate profit-and-loss view for each business line on demand.

9.4. Records shall be retained for the periods required by applicable tax and contractor-record-keeping law; the Member shall confirm retention periods with a CPA and a licensed Wisconsin attorney.

## ARTICLE 10. INDEMNIFICATION

10.1. To the fullest extent permitted by Wis. Stat. ch. 183, the Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member and the Manager from and against any liability, loss, or expense (including reasonable attorney fees) incurred in connection with the business of the Company, except for liability arising from the indemnified person's own willful misconduct, knowing violation of law, or conduct from which the person derived an improper personal benefit.

10.2. The Company may advance expenses to the extent permitted by law and may purchase insurance to support this indemnification obligation.

## ARTICLE 11. DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

11.1. The Company shall dissolve only upon the written election of the Member or as otherwise required by Wis. Stat. ss. 183.0701 to 183.0709.

11.2. On dissolution, the Company shall wind up its affairs, satisfy or make provision for its debts and liabilities (including any open construction obligations and warranties), and distribute any remaining assets to the Member.

## ARTICLE 12. AMENDMENT

12.1. This Agreement may be amended only by a writing signed by the Member. Because the Company is single-member, no other consent is required.

## ARTICLE 13. GOVERNING LAW AND VENUE

13.2. Any dispute relating to this Agreement that requires a forum shall be venued in Marathon County, Wisconsin, consistent with the venue and dispute-resolution provisions used in the Company's other agreements.

## ARTICLE 14. SEVERABILITY AND ENTIRE AGREEMENT

14.1. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions continue in effect.

14.2. This Agreement is the entire agreement of the Member concerning the governance of the Company and supersedes any prior operating agreement or understanding.
