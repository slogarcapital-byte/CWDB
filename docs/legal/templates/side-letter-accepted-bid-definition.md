# Side Letter: "Accepted Bid" Includes Company-Prepared Estimates

**Document version:** v1
**Drafted:** June 10, 2026
**Status:** DRAFT, not yet executed. One-time Wisconsin attorney review recommended before sending.
**Issue to:** each contractor separately (Barton Builders LLC; John Garcia Construction, LLC) using the bracketed placeholders.

---

## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Under the new operating model, CWDB (Jim) performs the lead follow-up, conducts the homeowner walk-through, and prepares the project estimate; the Contractor then signs the construction contract with the homeowner and performs the work. The original Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement assumed the Contractor would prepare its own bid. This one-page side letter closes the gap: it confirms that when a homeowner accepts a CWDB-prepared estimate for a project delivered to the Contractor, that acceptance counts as an "Accepted Bid" and triggers the $1,000 Lead Fee, exactly as if the Contractor had prepared the bid itself. Nothing else in the agreement changes.

## SIDE LETTER AGREEMENT

**Re:** Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement effective **[ORIGINAL AGREEMENT EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "Agreement"): clarification of "Accepted Bid"

Dear **[CONTRACTOR PRINCIPAL NAME]**:

This letter confirms our mutual understanding of how the Agreement applies when the Company, rather than the Contractor, prepares the project estimate.

1. **Company-prepared estimates.** From time to time the Company may respond to a Lead directly: contacting the homeowner, conducting the site walk-through, and preparing a written project estimate under the Company's name that identifies the Contractor as the contractor who will perform and contract for the work.

2. **Accepted Bid clarification.** The Parties agree that a homeowner's acceptance of a Company-prepared estimate described in Paragraph 1, for a project the Company has delivered to the Contractor, constitutes an "Accepted Bid" under the Agreement to the same extent as the homeowner's acceptance of a bid prepared by the Contractor. Acceptance occurs on the earlier of (a) the homeowner's signature on the estimate's acceptance block, or (b) the homeowner's execution of a construction contract with the Contractor for substantially the scope and price stated in the estimate (including any change orders agreed before signing).

3. **Lead Fee unchanged.** Each such Accepted Bid triggers the Lead Fee of One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) payable by the Contractor to the Company on the terms stated in the Agreement.

4. **Contractor's right to revise.** The Contractor is not obligated to contract at the Company's estimated price. If the Contractor and homeowner agree to a materially different scope or price, the resulting signed construction contract still constitutes an Accepted Bid for the same Lead, and no additional Lead Fee applies to that project.

5. **No other changes.** Except as stated above, the Agreement remains unchanged and in full force. This letter does not make the Company a party to any construction contract between the Contractor and a homeowner, and does not alter the independent-contractor relationship of the Parties.
