## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**What this contract does:** This is the agreement CWDB uses when it hires a subcontractor (Ben Barton, John Garcia, or a future sub) to perform deck building or staining work on a project where CWDB is the prime contractor and the homeowner is CWDB's customer. CWDB has contracted directly with the homeowner. CWDB then engages the sub to do the field work. The sub answers to CWDB, carries its own insurance, and indemnifies CWDB for the sub's own work.

**This is SEPARATE from the lead-purchase deal.** Ben Barton and John Garcia each signed a Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement (the pay-per-accepted-bid model where they buy leads from CWDB). **This Subcontractor Agreement does not replace, modify, or interact with that one.** Under the lead-purchase deal the contractor is the prime and the homeowner is the contractor's customer. Under this Subcontractor Agreement, CWDB is the prime and the homeowner is CWDB's customer, and the sub is paid by CWDB. The two relationships run on parallel tracks. Section 14 makes this explicit.

**Key terms at a glance:**

- Defines the specific project and the scope of subcontracted work
- Sets the price CWDB pays the sub and ties payment to lien waivers
- Requires a current certificate of insurance (general liability and workers' compensation or a valid exemption) naming CWDB as additional insured BEFORE the sub starts work
- Requires the sub to maintain any required Wisconsin licensing and to build to code
- Flows indemnification UP to CWDB for the sub's work
- Requires partial and final lien waivers as a condition of each payment
- Establishes independent-contractor status with anti-misclassification language
- Wisconsin governing law, Marathon County venue

**What Jim should know before using this template:**

1. **DO NOT LET A SUB START WORK WITHOUT A CURRENT COI NAMING CWDB AS ADDITIONAL INSURED.** This is the single most important protection. If a sub is injured or causes damage and is uninsured, the claim can land on CWDB as the prime. Section 3 makes the COI a condition precedent to starting work.

2. **MISCLASSIFICATION RISK.** Treating a sub as a true independent contractor (not an employee) under IRS common-law factors and Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development (DWD) standards matters for tax and workers' compensation liability. Section 7 is written to support independent-contractor status, but the day-to-day reality must match: do not control the means and methods of the sub's work, do not set their hours, and let them use their own tools and crew.

3. **COLLECT LIEN WAIVERS EVERY TIME YOU PAY.** A paid sub who never signs a waiver can still file a construction lien on the homeowner's property under Wis. Stat. ch. 779, which is a disaster for CWDB's reputation and a breach of CWDB's promise to the homeowner. Section 6 makes a signed lien waiver a condition of every payment.

4. **ATTORNEY REVIEW RECOMMENDED.** Have a licensed Wisconsin attorney confirm the insurance, indemnification, and independent-contractor provisions before first use.

**WHEREAS**, CWDB has entered into a home improvement contract with a homeowner to build, repair, or stain a deck (the "Prime Contract"); and

**WHEREAS**, CWDB wishes to engage the Subcontractor to perform certain of that work, and the Subcontractor wishes to perform it as an independent contractor;

**1.1 Project.** This Agreement covers the following project (the "Project"): homeowner [HOMEOWNER NAME], property at [PROJECT ADDRESS].

**1.2 Scope of Subcontracted Work.** The Subcontractor will furnish the labor, and (if stated) the materials, equipment, and supervision, to perform the following work at the Project (the "Subcontracted Work"): [DESCRIPTION OF SUBCONTRACTED WORK, including dimensions, materials, and finish/stain specifications].

**1.3 Standard of Work.** The Subcontractor will perform the Subcontracted Work in a good and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards, the Prime Contract specifications provided to the Subcontractor, and all applicable codes.

**1.4 Schedule.** The Subcontractor will begin on or about [SUB START DATE] and complete the Subcontracted Work on or about [SUB COMPLETION DATE], coordinating with CWDB.

### SECTION 2. PAYMENT

**2.1 Price.** CWDB will pay the Subcontractor [SUBCONTRACT PRICE] for the Subcontracted Work, [SELECT: as a fixed price / at the rate of [RATE]].

**2.2 Payment Schedule.** Payment will be made as follows: [PAYMENT SCHEDULE, for example progress payments tied to milestones and a final payment upon completion and acceptance].

**2.3 Conditions to Payment.** Each payment is conditioned on (a) CWDB's acceptance of the work to that point, and (b) the Subcontractor's delivery of a signed lien waiver under Section 6 covering the amounts being paid.

**2.4 Final Payment.** Final payment is conditioned on completion of the Subcontracted Work, correction of any punch-list items, delivery of a final lien waiver, and confirmation that the Subcontractor has paid its own workers and lower-tier suppliers.

**2.5 No Payment Obligation Beyond Scope.** CWDB is not obligated to pay for any work outside the Subcontracted Work unless CWDB authorized it in writing in advance.

---

### SECTION 3. INSURANCE (CONDITION PRECEDENT TO STARTING WORK)

**3.1 Required Coverage.** Before starting any work, and continuously throughout the Subcontracted Work, the Subcontractor shall carry, at its own expense:

(a) Commercial general liability insurance of at least One Million Dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence and Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000) general aggregate; and

(b) Workers' compensation insurance at Wisconsin statutory limits for all of the Subcontractor's employees, OR, if the Subcontractor has no employees and is exempt, a valid written exemption documenting that status under Wisconsin law.

**3.2 Additional Insured.** The Subcontractor's commercial general liability policy shall name **Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC as an additional insured** with respect to the Subcontracted Work.

**3.3 Certificate of Insurance Required Before Work.** The Subcontractor shall deliver to CWDB a current certificate of insurance (COI) evidencing the coverage in Section 3.1 and the additional-insured status in Section 3.2 **before starting any work on the Project.** CWDB has no obligation to allow the Subcontractor on site, and the Subcontractor shall not begin work, until CWDB has received and accepted the COI. The Subcontractor shall provide an updated COI on renewal and within five (5) business days of any change in carrier, limits, or coverage.

**3.4 Notice of Cancellation.** The COI shall provide for at least thirty (30) days' written notice to CWDB of cancellation, non-renewal, or material change.

---

### SECTION 4. LICENSING, PERMITS, AND CODE COMPLIANCE

**4.1 Licensing.** The Subcontractor represents and warrants that it holds, and will maintain throughout the Subcontracted Work, all licenses, registrations, and credentials required by the State of Wisconsin to perform the Subcontracted Work, including any applicable credential issued by the Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services (DSPS).

**4.2 Permits.** The Subcontractor will cooperate with CWDB on permits and inspections. Unless this Agreement states otherwise, CWDB (as prime) is responsible for the building permit, and the Subcontractor is responsible for any trade-specific permit its own work requires.

**4.3 Code Compliance.** The Subcontractor will perform the Subcontracted Work in compliance with the Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (Wis. Admin. Code SPS 320 through 325), including deck-specific structural requirements for footings, ledger attachment, guardrail height, and load rating, and with all applicable local codes and ordinances.

### SECTION 5. WARRANTY

**5.1 Subcontractor Warranty.** The Subcontractor warrants the Subcontracted Work against defects in workmanship for [SUB WARRANTY PERIOD, for example one (1) year] from completion, and will correct covered defects at no charge to CWDB. This warranty supports, and runs at least as long as, the warranty CWDB gives the homeowner under the Prime Contract.

**5.2 Materials.** The Subcontractor will pass through to CWDB all transferable manufacturer warranties on materials the Subcontractor supplies.

---

### SECTION 6. LIEN WAIVERS (CONDITION OF PAYMENT)

**6.1 Partial Lien Waivers.** As a condition of each progress payment, the Subcontractor shall deliver a partial lien waiver, in the form provided in CWDB's construction-lien package, waiving lien rights for amounts paid through the date of the waiver.

**6.2 Final Lien Waiver.** As a condition of final payment, the Subcontractor shall deliver a final lien waiver, in the form provided in CWDB's construction-lien package, waiving all lien rights for the Subcontracted Work.

**6.3 Lower-Tier Waivers.** If the Subcontractor uses its own lower-tier subcontractors or suppliers who may have lien rights under Wis. Stat. ch. 779, the Subcontractor shall obtain and deliver lien waivers from them as CWDB reasonably requests.

**6.4 No Liens.** The Subcontractor shall not file, and shall promptly discharge, any construction lien against the Project property for amounts CWDB has paid. A lien filed against a homeowner for paid work is a material breach of this Agreement.

---

### SECTION 7. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

**7.1 Independent Contractor.** The Subcontractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of CWDB. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint-venture, or agency relationship.

**7.2 Control of Means and Methods.** The Subcontractor controls the means, methods, sequence, and details of performing the Subcontracted Work. CWDB is interested in the result and may specify the result, scope, and schedule, but does not direct the day-to-day manner in which the Subcontractor performs the work.

**7.3 Tools, Crew, and Hours.** The Subcontractor provides its own tools, equipment, and crew, sets its own working hours within the agreed schedule, and may perform work for others.

**7.4 Taxes and Benefits.** The Subcontractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on amounts received, including self-employment taxes, and receives no employee benefits. CWDB will not withhold taxes and will issue a Form 1099-NEC for payments of $600 or more in a calendar year.

**7.5 Anti-Misclassification.** The Parties intend that the Subcontractor qualify as an independent contractor under the common-law factors applied by the Internal Revenue Service and under the standards applied by the Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development for unemployment and workers' compensation purposes. The Subcontractor is responsible for its own employees, including their wages, taxes, workers' compensation, and compliance with labor laws.

### SECTION 8. INDEMNIFICATION (FLOWING UP TO CWDB)

**8.1 Subcontractor Indemnification.** The Subcontractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless CWDB and its members, managers, employees, agents, and affiliates (the "CWDB Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all claims, actions, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to:

(a) The Subcontracted Work or the Subcontractor's performance or failure to perform;

(b) Bodily injury to or death of any person, or damage to property, to the extent caused by the negligent or wrongful acts or omissions of the Subcontractor or anyone for whom the Subcontractor is responsible;

(c) The Subcontractor's breach of this Agreement;

(d) The Subcontractor's violation of any law, code, or ordinance, including the Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (SPS 320 through 325) and local building codes; or

(e) The Subcontractor's failure to maintain required insurance or licensing, or any construction lien arising from the Subcontractor's work or lower-tier parties.

**8.2 Survival.** This indemnification survives completion of the Subcontracted Work and termination of this Agreement.

### SECTION 9. CONFIDENTIALITY

**9.1 Confidential Information.** The Subcontractor shall hold in confidence, and not use or disclose except to perform the Subcontracted Work, any non-public information of CWDB, including pricing, homeowner contact information, business methods, and the existence and terms of CWDB's homeowner contracts.

**9.2 Homeowner Information.** The Subcontractor shall use homeowner personal information only to perform the Subcontracted Work on the Project, and shall not market to, solicit, or sell the information of any CWDB homeowner. This obligation survives termination.

---

### SECTION 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

**10.1 Term.** This Agreement applies to the Project and continues until the Subcontracted Work is complete and finally paid, unless terminated earlier.

**10.2 Termination for Cause.** CWDB may terminate immediately on written notice if the Subcontractor fails to maintain required insurance or licensing, fails to deliver a required lien waiver, performs defective work and fails to cure within a reasonable time, or otherwise materially breaches this Agreement.

**10.3 Effect.** On termination, CWDB will pay for conforming work performed and accepted through the termination date, subject to lien waivers and any offset for CWDB's costs to complete or correct the work.

---

### SECTION 11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**11.1 Informal Resolution.** The Parties will first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith informal negotiation for thirty (30) days after written notice.

**11.2 Binding Arbitration.** If unresolved, either Party may submit the dispute to binding arbitration in Marathon County, Wisconsin, before a single arbitrator under the rules of the American Arbitration Association then in effect. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

**11.3 Equitable Relief.** Either Party may seek injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm without first completing the steps above.

### SECTION 14. RELATIONSHIP TO THE CONTRACTOR LEAD PURCHASE AGREEMENT

**14.1 Separate and Independent.** This Subcontractor Agreement is separate from, and does not amend, modify, supersede, or affect, any Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement between CWDB and the Subcontractor. Under that lead-purchase agreement, the Subcontractor (as "Contractor") buys homeowner leads from CWDB and is the prime contractor to those homeowners. Under this Subcontractor Agreement, CWDB is the prime contractor to the homeowner and engages the Subcontractor to perform work.

**14.2 No Cross-Default and No Offset.** A breach of one agreement is not automatically a breach of the other, and fees, payments, or penalties under one agreement are not offset against the other unless the Parties agree in a separate signed writing.

**14.3 Different Roles.** For clarity, in any given transaction the Parties occupy the role defined by the applicable agreement. The two relationships run in parallel and do not merge.
