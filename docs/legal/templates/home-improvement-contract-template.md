## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**What this contract does:** This is the agreement between Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC (CWDB) and a homeowner who hires CWDB directly to build or stain a deck. CWDB is now the prime contractor on the job. CWDB performs the work itself or through a subcontractor it hires (Ben Barton, John Garcia, or another sub under a separate subcontractor agreement). The homeowner pays CWDB. CWDB pays the sub. This is a different relationship from the lead-purchase business: here CWDB is the builder of record and carries the legal duties that come with that.

**Why this contract is built the way it is:** Wisconsin regulates home improvement work heavily through the Wisconsin Home Improvement Practices rule, Wis. Admin. Code ch. ATCP 110, and the consumer protection provisions of Wis. Stat. ch. 100. ATCP 110 requires that home improvement contracts be in writing, signed, and contain specific disclosures, a clear price, a description of the work, dates, and (for sales solicited at the buyer's home) a notice of the buyer's right to cancel. Violating ATCP 110 can be treated as an unfair trade practice and can expose CWDB to double damages plus attorney fees under Wis. Stat. 100.20(5). This template is designed to satisfy those requirements.

**Key terms at a glance:**

- Parties, project address, and a detailed scope of work with materials and dimensions
- A fixed total price and a progress payment schedule tied to milestones
- A start date and an estimated substantial-completion date
- A written change-order process (no extra work without a signed change order)
- A workmanship warranty plus pass-through of manufacturer warranties
- Who pulls permits and who pays for them
- A cleanup and debris-removal obligation
- A clear and conspicuous 3-day right to cancel that points to the attached Notice of Right to Cancel
- The ATCP 110 required statements and disclosures
- CWDB's insurance representation (commercial general liability)
- Wisconsin governing law and Marathon County dispute resolution

**What Jim should know before using this template:**

1. **CWDB IS THE PRIME CONTRACTOR HERE.** Unlike the lead-purchase model, in this contract CWDB takes on the legal duties of a home improvement seller and builder. That includes ATCP 110 compliance, the construction-lien Notice to Owner under Wis. Stat. 779.02(2) (see the lien package), and direct warranty liability to the homeowner. Confirm CWDB's commercial general liability insurance is active and adequate before signing any of these.

2. **THE RIGHT-TO-CANCEL NOTICE IS NOT OPTIONAL FOR HOME-SOLICITED SALES.** If this contract is signed at the homeowner's residence (or any place other than CWDB's regular place of business) and the price is above the regulatory threshold, the federal Cooling-Off Rule (16 CFR Part 429) and the Wisconsin counterpart require that you give the buyer two copies of a Notice of Right to Cancel and tell them about it. Failure to do so can let the buyer cancel indefinitely. Always attach and deliver the separate Notice of Right to Cancel template when the sale is solicited at the home.

3. **CONSPICUOUS-TYPE REQUIREMENTS.** ATCP 110 requires certain disclosures (notably the cancellation notice and certain warning language) to be clear and conspicuous, which in practice means bold and in a type size no smaller than the surrounding text and often at least 10 to 12 point. The sections below marked "CONSPICUOUS" must be set off in bold and printed at least as large as, and ideally larger than, the body text of the contract when this template is rendered to a signable document.

4. **ATTORNEY REVIEW RECOMMENDED.** Have a licensed Wisconsin attorney confirm the ATCP 110 disclosures, the payment schedule, the lien notice integration, and the warranty terms before first use.
