## PLAIN-ENGLISH HEADER: HOW WISCONSIN CONSTRUCTION LIENS WORK AND WHY WAIVERS MATTER

**The short version.** In Wisconsin, anyone who provides labor or materials to improve real estate (a prime contractor, a subcontractor, or a supplier) can claim a "construction lien" (also called a mechanic's lien) against the property if they are not paid. This is governed by Wis. Stat. ch. 779. A lien attaches to the homeowner's property, can cloud the title, and in a worst case can force a sale to satisfy the debt. The homeowner can end up paying twice if, for example, CWDB pays a subcontractor but cannot prove it, or if a sub or supplier never gets paid and files a lien.

**Two tools protect everyone, and CWDB must use both:**

1. **The Notice to Owner (Wis. Stat. 779.02(2)).** When CWDB is the prime contractor on a home improvement contract with an owner-occupant, Wisconsin law requires CWDB to give the owner a written Notice to Owner. The notice warns the owner that those who furnish labor or materials may have lien rights, and that the owner can protect itself by getting lien waivers. This notice is part (a) below. **Omitting it can cost CWDB its own lien rights and can be treated as a violation of the home improvement statute.**

2. **Lien Waivers (Wis. Stat. ch. 779).** Every time CWDB pays a subcontractor or supplier, CWDB should collect a signed lien waiver. A waiver is the paid party's signed statement that it gives up its lien rights for the amount paid. Collecting partial waivers as the job progresses, and a final waiver at the end, is how CWDB proves the chain of payment and keeps the homeowner's title clean. Parts (b) and (c) below are the waiver forms.

**Conditional vs. unconditional waivers.** A *conditional* waiver takes effect only once the payment actually clears (use it when paying by check that has not yet cleared). An *unconditional* waiver takes effect immediately on signing, whether or not payment clears (only sign or accept one when payment is truly in hand). Both variants are provided in part (b).

**When the Notice to Owner is required.** Provide it on every CWDB home improvement contract with an owner-occupant of an existing residence. It belongs in or attached to the homeowner contract. Provide it within the time and in the manner Wis. Stat. 779.02(2) requires (it must be in the contract itself or delivered to the owner within a short period after the contract is signed). There are narrow statutory exceptions (for example certain large projects or work on more than four family units), but for CWDB's typical single-family deck work, treat the Notice to Owner as mandatory and include it every time. **Consequence of omission:** failure to give the required notice can bar CWDB's own lien claim and undermines the homeowner protection the statute requires.

> **This Notice must be set out in CWDB's home improvement contract, or delivered to the owner as Wis. Stat. 779.02(2) requires. Print it clearly and conspicuously.**

**NOTICE TO OWNER**

**Project:** [PROJECT ADDRESS]
**Owner:** [HOMEOWNER NAME]
**Prime Contractor:** Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC, 906 N 16th Ave, Wausau, WI 54401, (715) 544-7941
**Date:** [DATE]

**As required by the Wisconsin construction lien law, Wis. Stat. 779.02(2), the prime contractor hereby notifies you, the owner, of the following:**

(a) AS A PART OF YOUR CONSTRUCTION CONTRACT, YOUR PRIME CONTRACTOR (CENTRAL WISCONSIN DECK BUILDERS, LLC) MAY BE REQUIRED TO PROVIDE LIEN WAIVERS FROM SUBCONTRACTORS, LABORERS, AND MATERIAL SUPPLIERS FOR YOUR PROTECTION.

(b) IF YOU DO NOT UNDERSTAND THESE REQUIREMENTS, OR THE STEPS TO BE TAKEN TO PROTECT YOURSELF FROM CONSTRUCTION LIENS, PLEASE CONSULT YOUR ATTORNEY.

The following statement is provided to comply with Wis. Stat. 779.02(2)(b):

"PLEASE READ THIS NOTICE. As a result of receiving services or materials for the improvement of your property, those who provide labor or materials for the work may have a right under Wisconsin law to claim a construction lien against your property if they are not paid. Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC and any subcontractors and material suppliers may have lien rights on your land and buildings if not paid. Those entitled to lien rights, in addition to Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC, are those who contract directly with you or those who give you notice within 60 days after they first furnish labor or materials for the work. Accordingly, you will receive notices from those who furnish labor or materials for the work, and you should give a copy of each notice you receive to your mortgage lender, if any. Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC agrees to cooperate with you and your lender, if any, to see that all potential lien claimants are duly paid."
