## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**What this contract does:** This is the agreement CWDB uses when it hires a subcontractor (Ben Barton, John Garcia, or a future sub) to perform deck building or staining work on a project where **CWDB is the prime contractor and the homeowner is CWDB's customer.** CWDB contracts directly with the homeowner. CWDB then engages the sub to do the field work. The sub answers to CWDB for the result, carries its own insurance, and indemnifies CWDB for the sub's own work.

**How it is structured (new in v1):** This is a **Master Subcontractor Agreement** that Ben or John signs ONCE. Each individual job is then added with a short one-page **Exhibit A Project Work Order** that names the CWDB Job Number, the homeowner, the address, the scope, the price, and the schedule. That means CWDB does not re-sign the whole contract for every deck. The Winchester job (August 17 to 21, 2026) is the first Exhibit A. A blank Exhibit A form and a filled Winchester example are at the end of this document.

**This REPLACES the old lead-purchase relationship.** Ben Barton and John Garcia each previously signed a Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement (the pay-per-accepted-bid model where they bought leads from CWDB). Those lead-purchase agreements are being **mutually terminated** (see `docs/legal/terminations/`). Going forward, the operative relationship is this Subcontractor Agreement, in which the roles are reversed: CWDB is the prime and pays the sub. Section 14 states this cleanly.

**Key terms at a glance:**

- Master agreement signed once; each job added by a short Project Work Order (Exhibit A) keyed to the CWDB Job Number.
- Requires a current certificate of insurance (commercial general liability and workers' compensation, or a valid exemption) naming CWDB as additional insured BEFORE the sub starts work.
- Requires the sub to hold and maintain any required Wisconsin licensing and to build to code (Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code, SPS 320 to 325).
- Flows indemnification UP to CWDB for the sub's work.
- Requires partial and final lien waivers as a condition of each payment (Wis. Stat. ch. 779).
- Establishes independent-contractor status with anti-misclassification language.
- Prohibits the sub from soliciting CWDB homeowners directly (protects CWDB's customer relationship).
- Wisconsin governing law, Marathon County venue.

**What Jim should know before using this template:**

1. **DO NOT LET A SUB START WORK WITHOUT A CURRENT COI NAMING CWDB AS ADDITIONAL INSURED.** This is the single most important protection. If a sub is injured or causes damage and is uninsured, the claim can land on CWDB as prime. Section 3 makes the COI a condition precedent to starting work. The Winchester start date (August 17) means the COI must be in hand well before then.
2. **CWDB'S OWN LICENSE MATTERS NOW.** Because CWDB is the prime contractor on a build, CWDB itself must hold the DSPS Dwelling Contractor Certification (entity) and Qualifier credential (Jim) before CWDB signs the homeowner and pulls the permit for the Winchester job. This subcontractor paper does not cure a missing CWDB license. Confirm the DSPS credentials are issued before the Winchester homeowner contract is signed.
3. **MISCLASSIFICATION RISK.** Treating a sub as a true independent contractor under IRS common-law factors and Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development standards matters for tax and workers' compensation liability. Section 7 supports independent-contractor status, but the day-to-day reality must match: do not control the means and methods, do not set the sub's hours, and let the sub use its own tools and crew.
4. **COLLECT LIEN WAIVERS EVERY TIME YOU PAY.** A paid sub who never signs a waiver can still file a construction lien on the homeowner's property under Wis. Stat. ch. 779. Section 6 makes a signed lien waiver a condition of every payment.
5. **ATTORNEY REVIEW.** Lauren Yde should confirm the insurance, indemnification, independent-contractor, and lien-waiver provisions before first use.

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, CWDB acts as a prime residential remodeling and deck contractor and enters into home improvement contracts directly with homeowners to build, repair, resurface, or stain decks;

**WHEREAS**, CWDB wishes to engage the Subcontractor from time to time to perform some or all of that field work as an independent contractor, on a project-by-project basis; and

**WHEREAS**, the Parties intend that this Agreement set the standing terms for all such projects, with each specific project added by a Project Work Order in the form of Exhibit A;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants below, the Parties agree as follows:

---

### SECTION 1. STRUCTURE, PROJECTS, AND SCOPE OF WORK

**1.1 Master Agreement Plus Work Orders.** This Agreement governs the overall relationship. No work is authorized until the Parties sign a Project Work Order in the form of **Exhibit A** for a specific project. Each signed Project Work Order incorporates this Agreement by reference and, together with this Agreement, forms the contract for that project. If a Project Work Order conflicts with this Agreement, this Agreement controls unless the Project Work Order expressly states that it modifies this Agreement for that project only.

**1.2 Project Identification.** Each project (a "Project") is identified by the CWDB Job Number (in the form CWDB-YYYY-NNN) assigned by CWDB, together with the homeowner name and property address stated in the Project Work Order.

**1.3 Scope of Subcontracted Work.** For each Project, the Subcontractor will furnish the labor, and (if the Project Work Order so states) the materials, equipment, and supervision, to perform the work described in the Project Work Order (the "Subcontracted Work"), including the dimensions, materials, and finish or stain specifications stated there.

**1.4 Standard of Work.** The Subcontractor will perform the Subcontracted Work in a good and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards, the specifications CWDB provides from its home improvement contract with the homeowner (the "Prime Contract"), and all applicable codes.

**1.5 Schedule.** The Subcontractor will begin and complete the Subcontracted Work on the dates stated in the Project Work Order, coordinating with CWDB. Time is of the essence with respect to the completion date stated in a Project Work Order.

---

### SECTION 2. PAYMENT

**2.1 Price.** For each Project, CWDB will pay the Subcontractor the amount stated in the Project Work Order, as a fixed price or at the rate stated there.

**2.2 Payment Schedule.** Payment will be made as stated in the Project Work Order, typically as progress payments tied to milestones and a final payment on completion and acceptance.

**2.3 Conditions to Payment.** Each payment is conditioned on (a) CWDB's acceptance of the work to that point, and (b) the Subcontractor's delivery of a signed lien waiver under Section 6 covering the amounts being paid.

**2.4 Final Payment.** Final payment is conditioned on completion of the Subcontracted Work, correction of punch-list items, delivery of a final lien waiver, and confirmation that the Subcontractor has paid its own workers and lower-tier suppliers.

**2.5 No Payment Beyond Scope.** CWDB is not obligated to pay for any work outside the Subcontracted Work unless CWDB authorized it in writing in advance by a signed change order.

**2.6 Back-Charge and Offset.** CWDB may offset against amounts otherwise due to the Subcontractor the reasonable cost to correct or complete defective or nonconforming work, or any other amount the Subcontractor owes CWDB under this Agreement, after giving the Subcontractor notice and a reasonable opportunity to cure where practicable.

**2.7 Payment Method.** CWDB pays by check or by card or digital payment. CWDB does not pay in cash.

---

### SECTION 3. INSURANCE (CONDITION PRECEDENT TO STARTING WORK)

**3.1 Required Coverage.** Before starting any work on a Project, and continuously throughout the Subcontracted Work, the Subcontractor shall carry, at its own expense:

(a) Commercial general liability insurance of at least One Million Dollars ($1,000,000) per occurrence and Two Million Dollars ($2,000,000) general aggregate; and

(b) Workers' compensation insurance at Wisconsin statutory limits for all of the Subcontractor's employees, OR, if the Subcontractor has no employees and is exempt, a valid written exemption documenting that status under Wisconsin law.

**3.2 Additional Insured.** The Subcontractor's commercial general liability policy shall name **Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC as an additional insured** with respect to the Subcontracted Work.

**3.3 Certificate of Insurance Required Before Work.** The Subcontractor shall deliver to CWDB a current certificate of insurance (COI) evidencing the coverage in Section 3.1 and the additional-insured status in Section 3.2 **before starting any work on a Project.** CWDB has no obligation to allow the Subcontractor on site, and the Subcontractor shall not begin work, until CWDB has received and accepted the COI. The Subcontractor shall provide an updated COI on renewal and within five (5) business days of any change in carrier, limits, or coverage.

**3.4 Notice of Cancellation.** The COI shall provide for at least thirty (30) days' written notice to CWDB of cancellation, non-renewal, or material change.

---

### SECTION 4. LICENSING, PERMITS, AND CODE COMPLIANCE

**4.1 Subcontractor Licensing.** The Subcontractor represents and warrants that it holds, and will maintain throughout the Subcontracted Work, all licenses, registrations, and credentials required by the State of Wisconsin to perform the Subcontracted Work, including any applicable credential issued by the Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services (DSPS).

**4.2 Permits.** Unless a Project Work Order states otherwise, CWDB (as prime) is responsible for the building permit for the Project, and the Subcontractor is responsible for any trade-specific permit its own work requires. The Subcontractor will cooperate with CWDB on permits and inspections and will not commence work requiring a permit before the permit is issued.

**4.3 Code Compliance.** The Subcontractor will perform the Subcontracted Work in compliance with the Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (Wis. Admin. Code SPS 320 through 325), including deck-specific structural requirements for footings, ledger attachment, guardrail height, and load rating, and with all applicable local codes and ordinances of the City of Wausau, Marathon County, or other governing jurisdiction for the Project.

**4.4 Lead-Safe Practices.** For work that disturbs painted surfaces on a residence built before 1978, the Subcontractor shall comply with the federal EPA Renovation, Repair, and Painting (RRP) Rule and any applicable Wisconsin requirements.

---

### SECTION 5. WARRANTY

**5.1 Subcontractor Warranty.** The Subcontractor warrants the Subcontracted Work against defects in workmanship for the period stated in the Project Work Order (and, if none is stated, for one (1) year) from completion, and will correct covered defects at no charge to CWDB. This warranty supports, and runs at least as long as, the warranty CWDB gives the homeowner under the Prime Contract.

**5.2 Materials.** The Subcontractor will pass through to CWDB all transferable manufacturer warranties on materials the Subcontractor supplies.

---

### SECTION 6. LIEN WAIVERS (CONDITION OF PAYMENT)

**6.1 Partial Lien Waivers.** As a condition of each progress payment, the Subcontractor shall deliver a partial lien waiver, in the form in CWDB's construction-lien package, waiving lien rights for amounts paid through the date of the waiver.

**6.2 Final Lien Waiver.** As a condition of final payment, the Subcontractor shall deliver a final lien waiver, in the form in CWDB's construction-lien package, waiving all lien rights for the Subcontracted Work.

**6.3 Lower-Tier Waivers.** If the Subcontractor uses its own lower-tier subcontractors or suppliers who may have lien rights under Wis. Stat. ch. 779, the Subcontractor shall obtain and deliver lien waivers from them as CWDB reasonably requests.

**6.4 No Liens.** The Subcontractor shall not file, and shall promptly discharge, any construction lien against the Project property for amounts CWDB has paid. A lien filed against a homeowner for paid work is a material breach of this Agreement.

---

### SECTION 7. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

**7.1 Independent Contractor.** The Subcontractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of CWDB. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint-venture, or agency relationship.

**7.2 Control of Means and Methods.** The Subcontractor controls the means, methods, sequence, and details of performing the Subcontracted Work. CWDB is interested in the result and may specify the result, scope, and schedule, but does not direct the day-to-day manner in which the Subcontractor performs the work.

**7.3 Tools, Crew, and Hours.** The Subcontractor provides its own tools, equipment, and crew, sets its own working hours within the agreed schedule, and may perform work for others.

**7.4 Taxes and Benefits.** The Subcontractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes on amounts received, including self-employment taxes, and receives no employee benefits. CWDB will not withhold taxes and will issue a Form 1099-NEC for payments of $600 or more in a calendar year.

**7.5 Anti-Misclassification.** The Parties intend that the Subcontractor qualify as an independent contractor under the common-law factors applied by the Internal Revenue Service and under the standards applied by the Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development for unemployment and workers' compensation purposes. The Subcontractor is responsible for its own employees, including their wages, taxes, workers' compensation, and compliance with labor laws.

---

### SECTION 8. INDEMNIFICATION (FLOWING UP TO CWDB)

**8.1 Subcontractor Indemnification.** The Subcontractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless CWDB and its members, managers, employees, agents, and affiliates (the "CWDB Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all claims, actions, liabilities, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to:

(a) The Subcontracted Work or the Subcontractor's performance or failure to perform;

(b) Bodily injury to or death of any person, or damage to property, to the extent caused by the negligent or wrongful acts or omissions of the Subcontractor or anyone for whom the Subcontractor is responsible;

(c) The Subcontractor's breach of this Agreement;

(d) The Subcontractor's violation of any law, code, or ordinance, including the Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (SPS 320 through 325) and local building codes; or

(e) The Subcontractor's failure to maintain required insurance or licensing, or any construction lien arising from the Subcontractor's work or lower-tier parties.

**8.2 Survival.** This indemnification survives completion of the Subcontracted Work and termination of this Agreement.

---

### SECTION 9. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-SOLICITATION OF CWDB HOMEOWNERS

**9.1 Confidential Information.** The Subcontractor shall hold in confidence, and not use or disclose except to perform the Subcontracted Work, any non-public information of CWDB, including pricing, homeowner contact information, lead sources, business methods, and the existence and terms of CWDB's homeowner contracts.

**9.2 Homeowner Information.** The Subcontractor shall use homeowner personal information only to perform the Subcontracted Work on the Project. This obligation survives termination.

**9.3 Non-Solicitation.** During the term of this Agreement and for one (1) year after the completion of a given Project, the Subcontractor shall not use its access to a CWDB homeowner obtained through that Project to solicit, divert, or perform additional deck or remodeling work for that homeowner outside of CWDB, except that this restriction does not apply to a homeowner with whom the Subcontractor had a pre-existing, independent relationship documented before the Project.

---

### SECTION 10. JOBSITE, SAFETY, AND CONDUCT

**10.1 Safety.** The Subcontractor is responsible for the safety of its own crew and for compliance with all applicable Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and Wisconsin safety requirements for its work.

**10.2 Jobsite Condition.** The Subcontractor shall keep its work area reasonably clean, remove its own debris, and protect the homeowner's property from damage caused by the Subcontractor's work.

**10.3 Homeowner Relations.** The Subcontractor shall conduct itself professionally on site and shall route any change in scope, price, or schedule through CWDB rather than agreeing to changes directly with the homeowner.

---

### SECTION 11. TERM AND TERMINATION

**11.1 Term.** This Agreement is effective on the Effective Date and continues until terminated. Either Party may terminate this Master Agreement for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice, except that termination does not affect any Project Work Order already signed and in progress, which continues to completion unless separately terminated.

**11.2 Termination for Cause.** CWDB may terminate this Agreement or any Project Work Order immediately on written notice if the Subcontractor fails to maintain required insurance or licensing, fails to deliver a required lien waiver, performs defective work and fails to cure within a reasonable time, or otherwise materially breaches.

**11.3 Effect.** On termination of a Project Work Order, CWDB will pay for conforming work performed and accepted through the termination date, subject to lien waivers and any offset for CWDB's costs to complete or correct the work.

---

### SECTION 12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**12.1 Informal Resolution.** The Parties will first attempt to resolve any dispute through good-faith informal negotiation for thirty (30) days after written notice.

**12.2 Binding Arbitration.** If unresolved, either Party may submit the dispute to binding arbitration in Marathon County, Wisconsin, before a single arbitrator under the rules of the American Arbitration Association then in effect. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

**12.3 Equitable Relief.** Either Party may seek injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm without first completing the steps above.

---

### SECTION 13. INSURANCE AND LIEN-WAIVER EXHIBITS

The certificate-of-insurance requirements in Section 3 and the lien-waiver forms in Section 6 are governed by CWDB's construction-lien package and certificate-of-insurance requirements, which are incorporated by reference (see `docs/legal/templates/lien-waiver-and-notice-to-owner.md`).

### SECTION 14. RELATIONSHIP TO THE TERMINATED CONTRACTOR LEAD PURCHASE AGREEMENT

**14.1 Prior Lead-Purchase Agreement Terminated.** The Parties acknowledge that any Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement previously entered into between CWDB and the Subcontractor (under which the Subcontractor bought homeowner leads from CWDB and paid CWDB a fee per accepted bid) has been, or is being, mutually terminated and released. This Agreement is not a continuation of that arrangement.

**14.2 Reversed Roles.** Under any prior lead-purchase arrangement, the Subcontractor was the prime contractor to the homeowner and paid CWDB. Under this Agreement, **CWDB is the prime contractor to the homeowner and pays the Subcontractor.** The two relationships are distinct, and there is no cross-default and no offset between them.

**14.3 No Revival.** Nothing in this Agreement revives, and this Agreement does not create, any lead-purchase, lead-referral, or pay-per-accepted-bid obligation.

**15.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Wisconsin, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

**15.2 Jurisdiction and Venue.** The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts in Marathon County, Wisconsin, for any matter not subject to arbitration.

**15.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the signed Project Work Orders, is the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the Subcontracted Work and supersedes prior discussions on that subject.

**15.4 Amendments.** Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

**15.5 Severability.** If any provision is unenforceable, it will be modified to the minimum extent necessary, and the rest remains in effect.

**15.6 No Waiver.** Failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver.

**15.7 Assignment.** The Subcontractor may not assign this Agreement or any Project Work Order without CWDB's written consent.

**15.8 Notices.** Notices must be in writing and delivered personally, by email with confirmation, or by certified mail to the addresses above.

**15.9 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures.** This Agreement and any Project Work Order may be signed in counterparts and by electronic signature.
