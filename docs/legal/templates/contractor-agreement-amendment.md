## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This is a short amendment to the existing Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement. It does three things and changes nothing else:

1. **Fixes the Company name** to "Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC, a Wisconsin limited liability company," correcting the pre-LLC name in the original v1 template, and confirms the LLC has assumed the agreement.
2. **Confirms in writing that CWDB may bid, contract for, and build deck projects itself**, including projects that came from its own leads, and that nothing in the lead-purchase agreement restricts CWDB from doing so.
3. **Clarifies that if the contractor works as a SUBCONTRACTOR for CWDB, that work is governed by a separate subcontractor agreement**, not by the lead-purchase agreement, and the two relationships are independent.

All other terms of the original agreement stay in force, including Wisconsin governing law and Marathon County arbitration.

### 1. DEFINED TERMS

Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Amendment have the meanings given to them in the Original Agreement. Except as expressly amended below, the Original Agreement remains in full force and effect.

### 2. CORRECTION OF COMPANY PARTY NAME AND ASSUMPTION BY THE LLC

**2.1 Name Correction.** All references in the Original Agreement to the Company, including "Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, a Wisconsin business operated by James Slogar," and any reference in the signature block, notices section, or Certificate of Insurance requirements to the Company by that name, are hereby amended to read **"Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC, a Wisconsin limited liability company."**

**2.2 Assumption.** Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC hereby assumes, ratifies, and agrees to perform all rights and obligations of the Company under the Original Agreement, as amended by this Amendment, as if the LLC had been the original named Company party. The Contractor consents to this assumption.

**2.3 Notices.** The Company's notice address under Section 16.8 of the Original Agreement is updated to: Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC, Attn: James Slogar, 906 N 16th Ave, Wausau, Wisconsin 54401, info@cwdeckbuilders.com.

### 3. COMPANY MAY BID FOR AND BUILD PROJECTS

**3.1 No Restriction on the Company.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that nothing in the Original Agreement restricts, limits, or prohibits the Company from bidding for, contracting with homeowners for, or performing deck construction, repair, replacement, resurfacing, expansion, staining, or improvement projects itself, including projects sourced from Leads that the Company generated. The Original Agreement contains no non-compete and no exclusivity provision binding the Company.

**3.2 No Implied Promise.** The Company makes no representation or warranty that it will refrain from performing such work or that it will deliver any particular Lead, or any particular volume or quality of Leads, to the Contractor. This Section 3 is consistent with and does not limit the Company's discretion under Sections 2.5 and 6.5 of the Original Agreement to pause, limit, redirect, or discontinue Lead delivery.

### 4. SUBCONTRACTED WORK GOVERNED BY A SEPARATE AGREEMENT

**4.1 Separate Relationship.** If the Contractor performs work for the Company as a subcontractor on a project for which the Company is the contracting party with the homeowner, that work is governed exclusively by a separate written subcontractor agreement between the Parties (the form of which is maintained by the Company at docs/legal/templates/subcontractor-agreement-template.md), and not by the Original Agreement.

**4.2 Independent Relationships.** The lead-purchase relationship under the Original Agreement (in which the Contractor pays the Company a Lead Fee for Accepted Bids) and the subcontractor relationship under any subcontractor agreement (in which the Company pays the Contractor for subcontracted work) are separate and independent. No payment, obligation, or default under one relationship shall be set off, netted, or applied against the other except by a separate written agreement signed by both Parties.

**4.3 No Conversion.** Nothing in this Amendment converts the Contractor into an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of the Company. The independent-contractor terms of Section 10 of the Original Agreement remain in effect for the lead-purchase relationship, and the subcontractor agreement governs the status of any subcontracted work.

### 5. NO OTHER CHANGES; RATIFICATION

**5.1 Continuing Effect.** Except as expressly amended by this Amendment, all terms and conditions of the Original Agreement remain in full force and effect and are hereby ratified and confirmed, including without limitation the Lead Fee (Section 4), the reporting obligation (Section 5), the verification and audit rights (Section 6), the independent-contractor status (Section 10), the limitation of liability (Section 13), the dispute resolution and binding arbitration in Marathon County, Wisconsin (Section 14), and the governing law of the State of Wisconsin (Section 16.1).

**5.2 Conflict.** In the event of any conflict between this Amendment and the Original Agreement, this Amendment controls solely as to the matters expressly addressed herein.

**5.3 Governing Law and Dispute Resolution.** This Amendment shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wisconsin, without regard to its conflict of laws principles, and is subject to the dispute resolution provisions of Section 14 of the Original Agreement, including binding arbitration in Marathon County, Wisconsin.

**5.4 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures.** This Amendment may be executed in counterparts, each of which is deemed an original and all of which together constitute one instrument. Electronic signatures are valid and binding to the same extent as original signatures.
