## PLAIN-ENGLISH EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**What this does:** This document cleanly ends the Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement between CWDB and John Garcia Construction, LLC (John Garcia). Neither side owes the other any money under it. Most importantly, it **kills the 12-month tail** in the original agreement, so if a homeowner from any old CWDB lead happens to sign with John in the next year, John owes CWDB nothing. Both sides walk away with a mutual release of claims.

**Why now:** CWDB pivoted on July 5, 2026, from selling leads to contractors toward performing deck work itself. The pay-per-accepted-bid lead model never produced a single accepted bid and CWDB never invoiced John a single dollar under it. The agreement is dead weight, and the tail clause is a lingering liability for both sides. Ending it also clears the deck for the new relationship: CWDB hiring John as a **subcontractor** on CWDB's own jobs (a separate agreement, see the subcontractor template).

**One important scoping note (John Garcia only):** CWDB has already paid John for subcontractor labor (for example the Garcia sub-labor invoice INV-2026-044, paid). That payment was under a subcontractor arrangement, NOT under the lead-purchase agreement being terminated here. This document settles only the lead-purchase agreement at $0. It does not touch, reopen, or claw back any subcontractor labor already paid, and it does not affect any future subcontractor work. Section 3.1 and Section 7 make this explicit.

**What Jim should know:**

1. **This settles the lead-purchase agreement at $0.** No Accepted Bid ever occurred under it, and CWDB never invoiced John a Lead Fee. Subcontractor labor payments are separate and unaffected.
2. **The tail is expressly waived** (Section 4). This is the whole point.
3. **Two protections deliberately survive:** John's confidentiality obligation (original Section 9) and his homeowner-data handling and deletion obligation (original Section 8).
4. **James Slogar signs personally too**, to close the pre-LLC personal-liability loop.

### RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, the Company and the Contractor entered into a Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement effective as of April 6, 2026, and executed by the Contractor on or about April 17, 2026 (the "Original Agreement"), under which the Company would deliver homeowner deck project leads to the Contractor and the Contractor would pay the Company a Lead Fee of One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) per Accepted Bid;

**WHEREAS**, the Original Agreement was prepared and signed using a template that identified the Company as "Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, a Wisconsin business operated by James Slogar," before the formation of Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC under Wisconsin Statutes Chapter 183, and the Company (as the LLC) and Slogar (individually) each wish to be bound by and to receive the benefit of this Termination Agreement so that the wind-down is complete regardless of which of them was the named "Company" party;

**WHEREAS**, since the effective date of the Original Agreement, no Accepted Bid has occurred, the Company has never invoiced the Contractor any Lead Fee, and no amount is or has ever become due from either Party to the other under the Original Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, separate from the Original Agreement, the Company has from time to time engaged, and may in the future engage, the Contractor as a subcontractor to perform field labor on the Company's own projects, and payments for such subcontractor labor are made under a subcontractor arrangement and not under the Original Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, on July 5, 2026, the Company adopted a business decision to prioritize performing residential deck construction, repair, resurfacing, and staining services itself, fed by the Company's own lead generation, and to treat the pay-per-accepted-bid lead-sale model as inactive;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to terminate the Original Agreement in full, settle all accounts under the Original Agreement at zero, extinguish the Tail Period and all ongoing reporting and payment obligations, and grant each other a mutual release, while preserving only the limited confidentiality and homeowner-data obligations identified below; and

**WHEREAS**, the Parties anticipate continuing and formalizing the separate Subcontractor relationship under a separate written Subcontractor Agreement, which relationship is independent of, and unaffected by, the termination of the Original Agreement;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual promises and releases set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

### SECTION 1. DEFINED TERMS

Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Termination Agreement (including "Accepted Bid," "Lead," "Lead Fee," "Tail Period," and "PII") have the meanings given to them in the Original Agreement.

### SECTION 2. TERMINATION OF THE ORIGINAL AGREEMENT

**2.1 Termination.** The Original Agreement is terminated in its entirety as of the Termination Effective Date. As of that date, the Original Agreement is of no further force or effect except for the limited surviving provisions expressly preserved in Section 6.

**2.2 No Further Lead Delivery.** The Company has no obligation to deliver, and the Contractor has no right to receive, any further Leads under the Original Agreement.

**2.3 Waiver of Notice Period.** The Parties waive the thirty (30) day notice requirement of Section 15.2 of the Original Agreement and agree that termination is effective immediately on the Termination Effective Date.

### SECTION 3. SETTLEMENT OF ACCOUNTS AT ZERO

**3.1 No Amounts Owed Under the Original Agreement.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that, as of the Termination Effective Date: (a) no Accepted Bid has ever occurred under the Original Agreement; (b) the Company has never issued an invoice to the Contractor under the Original Agreement; and (c) no Lead Fee, late fee, interest, penalty, liquidated damages, reimbursement, or other amount is or ever became due, owing, or payable by either Party to the other under the Original Agreement. For clarity, this Section addresses only the Original Agreement and does not affect, reopen, or claw back any payment already made, or any amount that may become due, for subcontractor labor performed by the Contractor under a separate subcontractor arrangement.

**3.2 Zero Balance.** Each of the Company, Slogar, and the Contractor confirms that the balance owed between them under the Original Agreement is zero dollars ($0.00), and that no payment is required by this Termination Agreement.

### SECTION 4. EXTINGUISHMENT OF THE TAIL PERIOD AND ONGOING OBLIGATIONS

**4.1 Tail Period Waived.** Notwithstanding Section 15.6 of the Original Agreement (the "Tail Period"), the Company waives, releases, and forever discharges any right to a Lead Fee, and the Contractor is forever released from any obligation to report or to pay a Lead Fee, with respect to any homeowner acceptance, contract, or Project occurring at any time on or after the Termination Effective Date, regardless of when the underlying Lead was delivered. There is no surviving tail.

**4.2 Reporting Obligation Waived.** The Contractor's reporting obligation under Section 5 of the Original Agreement, including the twelve (12) month ongoing reporting obligation under Section 5.3, is terminated and waived as of the Termination Effective Date.

**4.3 Audit and Verification Rights Waived.** The Company's verification, homeowner-follow-up, and audit rights under Section 6 of the Original Agreement, and the associated underreporting penalties and liquidated damages under Section 6.4, are terminated and of no further effect, except that nothing in this Section limits the surviving obligations preserved in Section 6 of this Termination Agreement.

### SECTION 5. MUTUAL RELEASE

**5.1 Release by the Contractor.** The Contractor, on behalf of itself and its members, managers, officers, employees, agents, successors, and assigns, hereby releases and forever discharges the Company, Slogar, and their respective members, managers, officers, employees, agents, affiliates, successors, and assigns from any and all claims, demands, damages, liabilities, obligations, costs, and causes of action of every kind, whether known or unknown, arising out of or relating to the Original Agreement or its subject matter, from the beginning of time through the Termination Effective Date.

**5.2 Release by the Company and Slogar.** The Company and Slogar, on behalf of themselves and their respective members, managers, officers, employees, agents, affiliates, successors, and assigns, hereby release and forever discharge the Contractor and its members, managers, officers, employees, agents, successors, and assigns from any and all claims, demands, damages, liabilities, obligations, costs, and causes of action of every kind, whether known or unknown, arising out of or relating to the Original Agreement or its subject matter, from the beginning of time through the Termination Effective Date.

**5.3 Scope of Release.** The releases in this Section 5 are intended to be general releases of all claims arising under or related to the Original Agreement. They do not release: (a) the obligations created by this Termination Agreement; (b) the surviving provisions preserved in Section 6; or (c) any obligation arising under any separate Subcontractor Agreement or other separate written agreement between the Parties, including any obligation relating to subcontractor labor.

**5.4 No Assigned Claims.** Each Party represents that it has not assigned or transferred, and will not assign or transfer, any claim released under this Section 5.

### SECTION 6. SURVIVING PROVISIONS

Notwithstanding the termination and releases above, the following obligations survive and continue in accordance with their terms:

**6.1 Confidentiality.** The Contractor's confidentiality obligations under Section 9 of the Original Agreement (protecting the Company's Confidential Information, including pricing, lead sources, systems, methods, and the identities of other contractors) survive for the two (2) year period stated in Section 9.4, measured from the Termination Effective Date.

**6.2 Homeowner Data Handling and Deletion.** The Contractor's obligations under Section 8 of the Original Agreement with respect to any Homeowner PII the Contractor received from the Company survive, including the permitted-use limits, the prohibition on resale or unrelated marketing, and the obligation under Section 8.4 to delete or destroy such PII (other than PII required for an active Project or by law) within thirty (30) days and to certify that deletion in writing to the Company. The Contractor shall provide the written deletion certification under Section 8.4 within thirty (30) days after the Termination Effective Date, or shall confirm in writing that it received no Homeowner PII from the Company.

**6.3 Mutual Non-Disparagement.** The mutual non-disparagement obligation under Section 11 of the Original Agreement survives for the one (1) year period stated therein, measured from the Termination Effective Date.

### SECTION 7. RELATIONSHIP GOING FORWARD

**7.1 Subcontractor Relationship Unaffected.** This Termination Agreement terminates only the Original Agreement. It does not create, limit, or affect any separate Subcontractor Agreement between the Parties, nor any payment for subcontractor labor already performed. The Parties acknowledge that the Company may engage the Contractor as a subcontractor on the Company's own projects under a separate written Subcontractor Agreement, and that such relationship is independent of the terminated lead-purchase relationship.

**7.2 No Continuing Lead Relationship.** After the Termination Effective Date, the Parties have no lead-purchase, lead-referral, or pay-per-accepted-bid relationship of any kind unless they enter into a new written agreement expressly establishing one.

### SECTION 8. NO ADMISSION

This Termination Agreement is a compromise and wind-down for business convenience. Nothing in it is or shall be construed as an admission of liability, wrongdoing, or breach by any Party.

### SECTION 9. REPRESENTATIONS AND AUTHORITY

Each Party represents and warrants that: (a) it has full power and authority to enter into this Termination Agreement; (b) the person signing on its behalf is duly authorized to do so; and (c) it has had the opportunity to consult independent legal counsel of its choosing before signing.

### SECTION 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**10.1 Governing Law.** This Termination Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Wisconsin, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

**10.2 Jurisdiction and Venue.** The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in Marathon County, Wisconsin.

**10.3 Entire Agreement.** This Termination Agreement, together with the surviving provisions of the Original Agreement expressly preserved in Section 6, constitutes the entire agreement among the Parties regarding the termination of the Original Agreement and supersedes all prior discussions on that subject.

**10.4 Amendments.** This Termination Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by all Parties.

**10.5 Severability.** If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary, and the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

**10.6 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures.** This Termination Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which is deemed an original and all of which together constitute one instrument.
