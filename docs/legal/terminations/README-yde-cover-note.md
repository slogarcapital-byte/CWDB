## Background in one paragraph

CWDB started as a lead-generation business: it sold homeowner deck leads to local contractors for $1,000 per accepted bid. Two contractors signed that Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement (LPA) on or about April 17, 2026 (effective April 6, 2026): **Ben Barton (Barton Builders LLC)** and **John Garcia (John Garcia Construction, LLC)**. That model never produced a single accepted bid and CWDB never invoiced either contractor one dollar. On July 5, 2026, CWDB pivoted to performing deck work itself as the prime contractor, fed by its own leads, and now wants to engage Ben and John as **subcontractors** on CWDB's own jobs. The first such job (Winchester) is scheduled August 17 to 21, 2026. We need the old paper cleanly ended and the new paper ready before that job.

## What each document does

1. **`mutual-termination-barton-2026-07.md`** and **`mutual-termination-garcia-2026-07.md`** — Mutual Termination and Release Agreements, one per contractor. Each: (a) terminates the LPA effective immediately; (b) settles all accounts at $0; (c) **expressly extinguishes the 12-month "Tail Period" (LPA Section 15.6) and the ongoing reporting obligation (LPA Section 5.3)**, so no future homeowner acceptance can trigger a fee; (d) grants a mutual general release; (e) preserves only the confidentiality (LPA Section 9), homeowner-data handling and deletion (LPA Section 8), and mutual non-disparagement (LPA Section 11) obligations; and (f) has James Slogar sign personally as well as for the LLC, to close the pre-LLC personal-liability loop (the LPAs were signed under the pre-LLC name "Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, a Wisconsin business operated by James Slogar").

2. **`../templates/subcontractor-agreement-v1.md`** — A Master Subcontractor Agreement (signed once per sub) plus a per-project Work Order (Exhibit A) keyed to the CWDB Job Number. CWDB is prime; the sub is paid by CWDB. Requires a COI naming CWDB as additional insured before any work, requires the sub to be licensed and to build to the Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code, flows indemnification up to CWDB, and conditions every payment on a lien waiver. It refines the earlier `subcontractor-agreement-template.md`, mainly by adding the master-plus-work-order structure, the CWDB Job Number tie-in, a non-solicitation clause, and Section 14, which reflects that the LPA is now terminated (rather than running in parallel). Winchester is the first Exhibit A.

## Questions we would like you to confirm

1. **Tail extinguishment.** Does Section 4 of each termination cleanly and enforceably kill the LPA Tail Period (Section 15.6) and ongoing reporting (Section 5.3) under Wisconsin law, or would you add belt-and-suspenders language (for example, a specific reference that no lead has ever converted and none is expected to)?
2. **Scope of the mutual release.** Is the general "known and unknown claims" release appropriate here, and is any Wisconsin-specific release-of-unknown-claims recital advisable? Should the release be limited to LPA-related claims only (as drafted) or broadened?
3. **James Slogar as an individual party.** We added Jim personally as a releasing and released party to neutralize the pre-LLC personal-liability exposure. Do you agree this is the right move, and is the drafting sufficient, or would you prefer a separate assumption or ratification instrument?
4. **Surviving provisions.** We kept confidentiality (2 years), homeowner-data handling and the Section 8.4 deletion certification (30 days), and mutual non-disparagement (1 year) alive. Do you want any of these released instead, or any added?
5. **Garcia subcontractor payment (Garcia doc only).** CWDB has already paid John Garcia for subcontractor labor (for example the paid Garcia sub-labor invoice). We scoped the $0 settlement to the LPA only and expressly carved out subcontractor labor (Garcia Sections 3.1 and 7.1). Please confirm that carve-out is clean and that the release cannot be read to waive or reopen paid or future subcontractor amounts.
6. **Consideration.** Each termination recites mutual releases as consideration and requires no payment. Is that adequate consideration in Wisconsin for a mutual termination and release, or should we add nominal consideration?
7. **Subcontractor agreement, licensing gate.** The subcontractor paper does not cure CWDB's own need to hold the DSPS Dwelling Contractor Certification (entity) and Qualifier (Jim) before CWDB signs the Winchester homeowner and pulls the permit. Please confirm CWDB is correctly positioned as prime for Winchester and flag anything missing on the CWDB-side licensing before August 17.
8. **Insurance and additional insured.** CWDB bound $1M/$2M commercial general liability around June 25, 2026. Section 3 of the subcontractor agreement requires the sub to carry the same and to name CWDB as additional insured before work. Please confirm the additional-insured and 30-day-cancellation-notice requirements are drafted the way you would want to see them, and whether you want a waiver of subrogation.
9. **Independent-contractor classification.** Given that Ben and John will do repeat work for CWDB, please confirm the independent-contractor and anti-misclassification language (Section 7) is sufficient under IRS common-law factors and Wisconsin DWD standards, and flag any operational practices Jim should follow to keep them true independent contractors.
10. **Non-solicitation.** Section 9.3 restricts a sub from soliciting a CWDB homeowner for a year after that homeowner's project. Please confirm this is enforceable and reasonable in scope under Wisconsin law (Wis. Stat. 103.465 restrictive-covenant standard, to the extent it applies to a business-to-business restraint).
