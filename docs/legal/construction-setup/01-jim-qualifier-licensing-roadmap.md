## Executive Summary (Plain English)

Wisconsin has no single "general contractor license." To legally build and remodel decks (and other work on one- and two-family dwellings) and pull your own permits, you need two DSPS credentials that work as a pair:

1. **Dwelling Contractor Qualifier** is YOUR credential (Jim, the person). You earn it by completing a 12-hour approved course and filing one form. No state exam.
2. **Dwelling Contractor Certification** is the LLC's credential. The LLC gets it by proving liability insurance and linking you as its Qualifier. No exam.

The good news: state fees are trivial (well under $100), the course is about $99 and self-paced online, and there is no exam to study for. The only real cost and the only real wait are your GL insurance premium (already in progress) and the few weeks DSPS takes to process the applications.

**The one optimization to act on now:** the 12-hour course does NOT require insurance. Start it this week in parallel with your GL policy. Insurance is only a prerequisite for the LLC's Certification, not for your individual Qualifier course or application.

**Bottom line sequence:** GL insurance (track A, in progress) and your 12-hour course (track B, start now) run in parallel, then both feed the LLC Certification filing, then local registration, then per-job permits.

## 1. The Two Credentials (and Why Both)

| | Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (DCQ) | Dwelling Contractor Certification |
|---|---|---|
| Who holds it | Jim (an individual) | The LLC (the business) |
| What it proves | A responsible person understands WI dwelling code, laws, and business practices | The business carries insurance and has a Qualifier behind it |
| How you earn it | 12-hour approved course (no exam), then file Form 3097 | File Form 3096, attach insurance proof, link Jim as Qualifier |
| Term | 2 years | 1 year |
| Renewal | $30 + 12 hours of approved CE per 2-year cycle | $25 per year, no CE |

The Certification cannot issue without (a) insurance in force and (b) a linked Qualifier. That is why Jim must hold the DCQ for the LLC's Certification to complete. Authority: Wis. Admin. Code SPS 305.315 (Qualifier) and SPS 305.31 (Certification); Wis. Stat. 101.654 (financial responsibility).

**Risk if you skip or get ahead of this: HIGH.** Pulling a residential permit, or signing a homeowner build contract as prime, without the Certification and bound insurance can trigger permit denial, stop-work orders, penalties, and an unenforceable contract. The current Phase 0 model (CWDB self-performs only cosmetic stain/resurface that needs no permit; any build/structural job is signed and permitted by a licensed builder) stays in force until both credentials issue and GL binds.

## 3. STEP 1 (Track A): Make the GL Policy DSPS-Ready (do this with your agent NOW)

Your GL policy is what unlocks the LLC Certification. To be accepted by DSPS, hand your agent these three requirements before the policy binds (baking them in now is cheaper than amending later):

1. **At least $250,000 per occurrence** in general liability. Your internal spec targets $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate, which clears the DSPS floor with room to spare. (Wis. Stat. 101.654(2).)
2. **Issued by an insurer authorized to do business in Wisconsin** (or an eligible surplus-lines insurer).
3. **A cancellation provision giving DSPS 30 days' prior written notice** of cancellation (10 days if for nonpayment of premium). DSPS requires this so the State is warned before coverage lapses.

Two more items for the agent, already flagged in `insurance-spec-sheet.md`:
- **Tell the carrier the LLC runs BOTH lead generation AND construction.** One LLC, two business lines. If the construction classification is not disclosed, coverage can be voided.
- **Bond alternative (only if you ever go that route):** the statute allows a surety bond instead of insurance. A bond under $25,000 forces a "restricted" certification (you cannot take jobs whose estimated cost exceeds the bond). Insurance is the cleaner path; the bond note is here only for completeness.

> Proof of insurance is uploaded inside the LicensE application in Step 4. Confirm the policy effective date lands on or before the day you file the Certification.

## 4. STEP 2 (Track B): Complete the 12-Hour Qualifier Course (start this week)

DSPS requires "at least 12 hours in an approved initial qualifier course in dwelling construction, completed within one year prior to the date of application." The course covers construction laws, codes, and business practices, and carries its own tests. There is no separate state exam.

## 5. STEP 3: File Jim's Dwelling Contractor Qualifier Application

- **Where:** the DSPS LicensE portal, https://license.wi.gov (create an account if you do not have one).
- **Form:** 3097 (Dwelling Contractor Qualifier Application Information). Also have Form 3198 (Trades LicensE Information) and Form 2255 (Convictions and Pending Charges, only if any apply) on hand.
- **Attach:** evidence of the completed 12-hour course.
- **Fee:** the state credential fee is small (the DCQ renews at $30 per 2-year cycle; the initial application fee is collected in LicensE and is in the same modest range). Confirm the current amount in LicensE at filing.
- **No insurance needed for this step.** File it as soon as the course is done, even if GL has not bound yet.
- **Processing:** if DSPS requests more documents, you have 90 days to respond or the application is abandoned (and fees must be repaid). Inquiries are answered within about 3 business days.

## 6. STEP 4: File the LLC's Dwelling Contractor Certification

File this once BOTH the GL policy is bound (Step 1) and Jim's DCQ is filed/issued (Step 3), so you can link him as the Qualifier.

- **Where:** LicensE, https://license.wi.gov.
- **Form:** 3096 (Dwelling Contractor Certification Application Information). Form 2255 if applicable.
- **Attach:** the certificate of insurance / policy showing the $250,000+ limit and the 30-day DSPS cancellation notice.
- **Link the Qualifier:** the Certification requires the business to "engage, as an employee, a person who holds a Dwelling Contractor Qualifier certification." For a single-member LLC, that is Jim.
- **Fee:** entity credential renews at $25 per year; the initial application fee is collected in LicensE and is modest. Confirm in LicensE.
- **Processing:** same 90-day document-response window as Step 3.

## 8. STEP 5: Confirm Local Registration (Wausau and Marathon County)

State law (Wis. Stat. 101.654(4)) bars local governments from imposing stricter insurance, education, or exam rules than the state. But a municipality may still require a **local contractor registration** before it will issue permits. Verify per jurisdiction; do not assume.

- **Inside City of Wausau:** confirm with the City of Wausau Building Inspection Department whether a local contractor registration is required, and apply for permits through the city portal (https://evolveportal.ci.wausau.wi.us).
- **Towns outside city limits (and parts of Schofield, Weston, Mosinee, Merrill):** confirm the issuing office with Marathon County's building inspection program or the town's designated inspection agency.
- **Per-job permit:** a new deck or a structural alteration needs a building permit; cosmetic re-staining of a sound deck generally does not. Build to the SPS 321 prescriptive tables (ledger, footings, guardrail height and spacing, joist/beam sizing). See the companion guide Sections 3 and 4.
