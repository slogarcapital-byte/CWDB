## Executive Summary (Plain English)

Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, LLC (CWDB) currently runs one business line: generating homeowner deck leads and selling them to contractors. This initiative stands up a SECOND business line under the SAME LLC: CWDB performing deck work directly (new builds, repairs, replacements, resurfacing, staining, and refinishing). Both lines will run side by side.

The locked decisions driving this roadmap:

1. Construction runs UNDER the existing CWDB LLC. No separate entity is being formed right now.
2. Jim starts solo as owner-operator. As job volume grows, contractor partners Ben Barton (Barton Builders LLC) and John Garcia (John Garcia Construction, LLC) convert from lead buyers into SUBCONTRACTORS.
3. Lead generation and direct building operate together, not as a replacement.

Because both lines live in one entity, the legal protections that normally come from a separate company have to be recreated by other means: real commercial insurance, an operating agreement that authorizes both lines, and strictly separate bookkeeping. Those three substitutes are the backbone of this roadmap. See the entity-structure-and-spinout-memo.md for the full liability analysis (rated HIGH).

This document is the single front door. Every other deliverable in the initiative is indexed below with its path, owner, rough cost, and dependencies.

## Real-World Execution Order

This is the order to actually do the work. It is not strictly the same as the index numbering, because some legal and insurance steps gate others.

1. **Adopt the operating agreement** (`docs/legal/cwdb-operating-agreement.md`). This authorizes both business lines and locks in the separation and insurance covenants that protect the liability shield. Do this first because it is the governance foundation everything else relies on.

2. **Bind insurance FIRST.** Commercial general liability must be in force, and workers' compensation must be arranged if and when labor is added. This comes before licensing because the Wisconsin Dwelling Contractor Certification requires proof of insurance as a condition of issuance. See `docs/legal/construction-setup/insurance-spec-sheet.md`. Workers' compensation in Wisconsin is governed by Wis. Stat. ch. 102; verify your threshold with the Wisconsin Department of Workforce Development or a licensed Wisconsin attorney.

3. **Apply for the Wisconsin credentials.** Complete the 12-hour Dwelling Contractor Qualifier course, obtain the Dwelling Contractor Qualifier credential, and obtain the Dwelling Contractor Certification at WI DSPS. Then complete any local City of Wausau and Marathon County contractor registration and permit prerequisites. See `docs/legal/construction-setup/wisconsin-licensing-guide.md`. Dwelling contractor credentialing is governed by Wis. Stat. ch. 101 and Wis. Admin. Code ch. SPS 305; verify current course hours and fees with WI DSPS.

4. **Put the homeowner-facing documents in use before signing the first job.** That means the home improvement contract (`docs/legal/templates/home-improvement-contract-template.md`), the notice of right to cancel (`docs/legal/templates/notice-of-right-to-cancel.md`), the change order template (`docs/legal/templates/change-order-template.md`), and the lien waiver and notice to owner (`docs/legal/templates/lien-waiver-and-notice-to-owner.md`). Wisconsin home improvement practices are governed by Wis. Admin. Code ch. ATCP 110; the right to cancel for in-home sales is governed by Wis. Stat. s. 423.203 and ATCP 110.

5. **Before subbing any work to Ben or John:** sign the subcontractor agreement (`docs/legal/templates/subcontractor-agreement-template.md`), collect a current certificate of insurance from each subcontractor naming CWDB as additional insured, and execute the lead purchase agreement amendment (`docs/legal/templates/contractor-agreement-amendment.md`) that converts their relationship and addresses the channel conflict.

6. **Confirm tax and money handling.** Confirm sales/use tax treatment with the Wisconsin Department of Revenue (Wisconsin sales and use tax is governed by Wis. Stat. ch. 77 and Wis. Admin. Code ch. Tax 11), set up separate bookkeeping for the lead-gen line versus the construction line per the operating agreement, and decide the S-Corp election before about 2026-06-20. See `docs/legal/construction-setup/tax-treatment-memo.md`.

7. **Make the website honest.** Update the site so that "licensed and insured" is true of CWDB only after Steps 2 and 3 are actually complete. Separately, resolve the two pre-existing HIGH-risk blockers already flagged in legal memory: the fabricated AI testimonials (FTC 16 CFR Part 255 and the FTC rule on fake reviews) and the missing privacy policy. Do not let the new construction launch ride on top of unresolved advertising-compliance problems.

## Requires a Human / Out of Scope

The following items cannot be completed by drafting documents. They require Jim plus a licensed professional and are out of scope for this drafting initiative:

- **Actual government filings.** Submitting the Dwelling Contractor Qualifier and Certification applications, paying credential fees, and completing local Wausau/Marathon County registration. Requires Jim; verify all current fees and requirements with WI DSPS and local offices.
- **Binding insurance.** Only a licensed insurance broker can bind commercial general liability and workers' compensation coverage. The spec sheet describes the target coverage; it does not place coverage.
- **S-Corp election filing.** The decision and the IRS Form 2553 filing are a CPA matter and must be decided before about 2026-06-20. This roadmap flags the deadline; it does not make the election.
- **Final legal sign-off.** Every template and memo in this initiative should be reviewed by a licensed Wisconsin attorney before execution. Tax positions should be confirmed by a CPA. Insurance adequacy should be confirmed by a broker.
