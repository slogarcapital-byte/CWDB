# SBG Construction Group: Partnership Package

Everything for the SBG Construction Group structure (Slogar, Barton, Garcia). Rebuilt 2026-06-17 after the partner meeting chose a shared-services structure instead of a full merger.

## The structure in one paragraph

The three existing LLCs (Jim's Central Wisconsin Deck Builders, Ben's Barton Builders, John's John Garcia Construction) stay independent and keep signing their own jobs and keeping their own profit. SBG is a shared group of three companies, owned one third each by the three individuals, that holds the W-2 crews (SBG-Labor), the equipment (SBG-Equipment), and later the real estate (SBG-RealEstate, dormant for now), and rents them to the three LLCs at market rates. Phase A (now) is this co-op. Phase B (1 to 2 years out, only if it works) is a true merger where job profit pools and splits one third, on rules we set now.

## The decisions these documents recommend (so you can defend them)

1. **Entities:** three separate SBG companies, owned one third each by the three of you as individuals. SBG-Labor as an S-corp (so you can draw a real W-2 wage), SBG-Equipment and SBG-RealEstate as partnership LLCs. Real estate dormant for now.
2. **Money:** equal cash in. SBG bills the LLCs at market rates and reinvests its profit in Phase A. Each partner earns their own LLC's job profit plus an SBG wage. No pooling until Phase B.
3. **What each keeps:** each partner keeps their own customers, brand, and lead sources. SBG does not market or own customer relationships. Any prior lead-sharing arrangement among the partners is wound down.
4. **Phase B:** decide the trigger and the valuation and equalization method NOW, in the term sheet, while everyone is aligned.

## Hard gates and open items (confirm before relying on this)

- A licensed Wisconsin business attorney drafts the operating agreements, the three inter-company agreements, and the buy-sell.
- The CPA confirms the tax setup, especially the **SBG-Labor S-corp election** (a partner cannot be a W-2 employee of a partnership, so this is what makes the wages work).
- The **Wisconsin DOR** confirms sales tax on the equipment leases (renting equipment between your entities is a taxable rental). SBG-Equipment registers and remits.
- **DSPS:** each operating LLC keeps its own Dwelling Contractor Certification, Qualifier, and GL. Confirm SBG needs no contractor cert as a lessor, and confirm the leased-labor workers' comp interaction.
- Size the **equal cash** against a real equipment list and payroll-float estimate (working range $30,000 to $45,000 each).
