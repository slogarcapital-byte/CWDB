## OVERVIEW
New fence installation at the project address: approximately <b>{fence_lf} linear feet</b> of <b>{fence_height} ft {fence_material}</b> fence{fence_color_phrase}{gates_phrase}. Posts are set in concrete to below the Wisconsin frost line and the fence is built to follow the staked property line and grade. Work includes layout, site protection, and a final homeowner walkthrough.

## SCOPE
- Verify the property line and stake the fence run with the homeowner before any digging
- Call in a public utility locate (Diggers Hotline) and hand-dig near any marked lines{tearout_scope_line}
- Set posts in concrete footings below the Wisconsin frost line at code/manufacturer-required spacing
- Install {fence_height} ft {fence_material} panels/sections, plumb and to consistent height, following grade
{gates_scope_line}- Final cleanup and haul-off of all construction debris
- Final homeowner walkthrough and punch-list resolution

## SCOPE_NOTE
The scope above assumes the homeowner has confirmed the property line (a survey is the homeowner's responsibility if the line is in question), standard soil with no buried obstructions or ledge rock, and no underground utilities within the fence line beyond what Diggers Hotline marks. Rock, buried debris, or undisclosed utilities discovered during post-hole digging will be discussed before continuing, and any change order will be priced separately and added in writing.

## INCLUDED
- All materials: {fence_material} panels/sections, posts, concrete, gates, and hardware
{tearout_included}- All labor for layout, post setting, panel installation, and gates
- Permit allowance where a fence permit is required (homeowner named on permit)
- Final job-site cleanup and haul-off of construction debris
- <b>1-year limited workmanship warranty</b> on installation
- Manufacturer warranties pass through on the fence material

## NOT_INCLUDED
- Property-line survey or staking of a disputed line
- Removal of rock, ledge, stumps, or buried debris (quoted separately if found)
- Grading, drainage, or retaining work to level a sloped run beyond standard stepping/raking
- Restoration of landscaping, sod, or irrigation disturbed along the fence line beyond basic backfill and tamp
- Electrical for gate operators or low-voltage lighting (separate trade required)
- HOA or municipal approvals (homeowner responsibility); CWDB builds to the approved height/style
- Snow removal during winter installs
