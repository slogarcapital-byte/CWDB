# Phone Call Script — Ben + John (2026-04-27)

## Numbers (from HubSpot, verified 2026-04-27)

- **John Garcia (John Garcia Construction, LLC):** (715) 567-0269

Call from your personal cell, not the (715) 544-7941 business line — feels personal, not "the company calling."

Call Ben first (he replied first historically). If voicemail, leave the voicemail script below and try John. Loop back to Ben in 2 hours if no callback.

---

## Tone

Warm. Casual. Not "checking in." Not "following up." You're a partner calling a partner. You're not asking for permission — you're asking for the smallest yes.

**Time target: 60–90 seconds.** Get in, get the ask, get out. Long calls feel like sales calls.

---

## Opening (10 seconds)

> "Hey Ben — Jim Slogar from Central Wisconsin Deck Builders. You got a quick second?"

If "no" → "No worries, when's a better time today or tomorrow?" — get a window. Hang up.

If "yeah, what's up" → continue.

---

## The bridge (20 seconds)

> "So — quick context. We're going live with ads this week, and I sent you a text last Monday asking for a deck photo and a homeowner quote. Totally get if it slipped through — I know you've been busy. Wanted to call so it's easier than digging through texts."

You're explicitly giving them the out ("totally get if it slipped through"). They don't have to apologize. Removes friction.

---

## The ask — pick the smallest yes (30 seconds)

Offer a ladder. Take whatever rung they accept.

> "Three options, easiest to hardest. Pick whatever works:
>
> **One** — just send me a phone photo of any deck you've finished recently. That's it. I'll handle everything else.
>
> **Two** — give me a homeowner's name and number, I'll call them and write up a one-sentence testimonial for you both to approve.
>
> **Three** — text me one line you remember a customer saying. I'll attribute it as 'Wausau homeowner' if they don't want a name on it.
>
> Which of those sounds easiest?"

**Why this works:** The three-option ladder makes "no" awkward. "Just a phone photo" is the smallest yes — almost no one says no to that. Option 2 is the highest-value because it generates a real testimonial without burdening them. Option 3 is the floor.

---

## Closing (10 seconds)

Whichever option they pick:

> "Perfect. Text it to me at (715) 544-7941 or email info@cwdeckbuilders.com — whichever's easier. I need it by **end of day Wednesday (2026-04-29)** so we can launch with it. Thanks Ben, talk soon."

The deadline is critical. Without it, "this week" becomes next week. Wednesday gives them 48 hours, you 24 hours to upload before the un-pause.

---

## If they push back

### "We're slammed right now."
> "Totally hear you. That's why I'm asking for the photo — literally five seconds. You're texting me one image. I'll do the rest."

### "I don't have any good photos."
> "Not looking for pro-grade. Phone photos are actually better — they look authentic. Even one of a deck from your driveway works."

### "My customers don't do testimonials."
> "Got it. Let's do option 1 then — just the photo. We can build testimonials later as new jobs come through our leads."

### "Are leads actually coming yet?"
> "Honest answer: ads went live Thursday but I paused them Saturday for a tracking fix. Re-launching this week. The proof on the site is the difference between leads that close and leads that ghost — that's why I'm calling. It's the cheapest move I have to raise your close rate before traffic hits."
