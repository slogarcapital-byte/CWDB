The board gated the A-tier calls on answering: why did 8 June estimates die? Answer from the warehouse: **June was not a 0-for-8 sales failure. It was 3 data artifacts, 4 license-gated big builds with no follow-up, and 1 real competitive loss.**

Note the pattern: six of the eight were stamped "bid sent" on the SAME day (6/19), and the deaths cluster on 7/6, 7/10, 7/13: the July data-cleanup days. Much of June's "0% close rate" is bookkeeping, not homeowners saying no.

## The three real killers (in order)

1. **The license gate.** Every real June estimate was a $9k-$30k build/replacement that CWDB could not sign pre-license. The only job ever closed (Overbeck) was the only one the license did not gate. This is the chairman's point made empirical: the course IS the sales fix.
2. **Silence after the send.** Zero follow-up touches on any of them. Kampstra (structurally literate, high intent) got nothing for 24 days. The 7/14/21-day cadence shipped 7/22 exists precisely for this; it must actually run.
3. **Speed and expectation-setting.** 12-16 days lead-to-bid on the 6/19 batch, and Reist got a bid at 3x his stated budget with no conversation. Both now have guardrails (notify path + /bid budget check) but the guardrails only matter on the next cohort.

## What this changes for the calls (per the board)

- The apology-opener stands, and it is honest: these people heard nothing after asking. For Wroblewski and Neely the truthful line is that we never actually got them a number and want to fix that.
- Lead with the stain/repair profile where CWDB can sign TODAY (Hanson, Nelson-Claeys, Peksa). For build-scoped leads (Reist re-approach, Wroblewski if build), book the walk-through now and be explicit: "we start builds once the state paperwork clears in August," which is a schedule statement, not a dodge.
- Kampstra and Johnson are re-open candidates, not corpses: one honest call each ("marked our books wrong in a cleanup; are you still deciding?") costs nothing.
- Fix the three stage mislabels in HubSpot while on each call (Hanson especially).

## Verdict for the board record

June's close rate measured the license gap plus record hygiene, not market rejection. The two fixes are already in motion: the DSPS course (releases the gated segment) and the follow-up cadence (kills the silence). No pricing change is warranted from this cohort except enforcing the budget-vs-bid check that Reist proves necessary.
