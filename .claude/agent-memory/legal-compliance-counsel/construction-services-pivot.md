**Verified DSPS licensing specifics (2026-06-24; resolves the "verify with DSPS" placeholders, executable roadmap at `docs/legal/construction-setup/01-jim-qualifier-licensing-roadmap.md`):**
- Two credentials, NO Wisconsin general-contractor license exists, NO exam (the course carries its own tests). Authority: Wis. Stat. 101.654, Wis. Admin. Code SPS 305.31 (entity Certification) + SPS 305.315 (Qualifier). File via the DSPS LicensE portal, license.wi.gov.
- Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (Jim, the person): 12-hour approved initial course completed within 1 year before applying (~$99 online self-paced; approved providers include Wisconsin Contractors Institute, @-Leisure, @ Your Pace, RocketCert, and Madison College for in-person). Form 3097. Term 2 years; renews $30 + 12 hrs CE per cycle ($25 late-CE fee, $200 reinstatement). The course has NO insurance dependency, so it runs in PARALLEL with GL binding, not after it.
- Dwelling Contractor Certification (the LLC): Form 3096; must engage a DCQ as an employee (= Jim); term 1 year; renews $25, no CE; $200 reinstatement. Requires insurance proof + a solo workers-comp exemption attestation.
- Insurance DSPS requires to issue the Certification: at least $250,000 per occurrence GL (CWDB's $1M/$2M spec clears it), a WI-authorized insurer, and a 30-day cancellation-notice-to-DSPS provision (10 days for nonpayment). Bond alternative is at least $5,000 (under $25k forces a "restricted" cert). Hand these three features to Jim's insurance agent BEFORE the policy binds.
- Local: Wis. Stat. 101.654(4) preempts stricter local insurance/education/exam rules, but City of Wausau / Marathon County may still require a local contractor registration; confirm per jurisdiction.
