## Key drafting decisions (durable)
- **Tail kill:** each termination Section 4 expressly extinguishes the LPA Tail Period
  (LPA Sec. 15.6) AND the 12-month ongoing reporting obligation (LPA Sec. 5.3). This is
  the whole point; an ordinary termination would leave the tail alive.
- **$0 settlement** scoped to the LPA only. Garcia doc carves OUT already-paid
  subcontractor labor (e.g. INV-2026-044 $800) in Sec. 3.1 + 7.1 so the release cannot
  reopen or claw it back.
- **James Slogar signs personally** on both terminations (LPAs were signed under the
  pre-LLC name "CWDB, a Wisconsin business operated by James Slogar"); this neutralizes
  the pre-LLC personal-liability exposure. Belt-and-suspenders alongside the LLC signature.
- **Surviving from the LPA:** confidentiality (Sec. 9, 2 yrs), homeowner-data handling +
  Sec. 8.4 deletion certification (30 days), mutual non-disparagement (Sec. 11, 1 yr).
  Everything else released.
- **Subcontractor v1 structure:** Master Agreement signed once per sub + per-project
  Exhibit A Work Order keyed to the CWDB Job Number (dim_jobs, CWDB-YYYY-NNN). Winchester
  (Aug 17-21 2026) is the first Exhibit A; job number left as a fill-in (do NOT assume;
  next was 045 as of 2026-06-26). Section 14 reflects the LPA is TERMINATED (not parallel).
  COI naming CWDB additional insured is a condition precedent to work; lien waivers gate
  every payment; non-solicitation of CWDB homeowners added (Sec. 9.3).
