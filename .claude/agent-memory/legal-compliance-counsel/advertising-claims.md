## Claim 1: "Free Quotes"
**Status:** APPROVED — LOW RISK (with conditions)

The claim is truthful: homeowners pay nothing for quotes. This is permissible under FTC Act Section 5 and Wis. Stat. 100.18 (deceptive advertising).

**Conditions:**
- Quotes must genuinely be free with no hidden charges to homeowners
- No obligation can be imposed as a condition of receiving a quote
- If CWDB ever changes this (e.g., requiring a deposit), the claim must be immediately removed

## Claim 2: "Licensed and Insured Builders"
**Status:** APPROVED — MEDIUM RISK (with conditions)

The claim is truthful IF all contractor partners actually hold valid Wisconsin contractor licenses and current general liability insurance.

**Conditions CWDB must maintain:**
1. Verify each contractor's license status before onboarding (check Wisconsin DSPS credential lookup)
2. Collect and verify current Certificate of Insurance (COI) from each contractor
3. Re-verify annually or upon policy expiration
4. If ANY contractor partner is unlicensed or uninsured, the claim must be qualified (e.g., "Our builders carry their own licenses and insurance" with verification language)
5. Include in Contractor Lead Purchase Agreement: contractor represents and warrants they maintain valid WI contractor license and GL insurance; must notify CWDB within 5 days of lapse

**Why MEDIUM risk:** CWDB does not directly control contractor licensing/insurance. If a contractor's license lapses and CWDB continues advertising "licensed and insured," that becomes a false claim under Wis. Stat. 100.18. The mitigation is contractual verification + periodic checks.

## Ad Copy Legal Review Gate
**Rule (established 2026-04-04):** ALL ad copy — Google Ads, Facebook/Instagram, Nextdoor, TikTok — must be reviewed by legal counsel before going live. No exceptions.

**Why:** CWDB's advertising creates potential liability under FTC Act, Wis. Stat. 100.18, and platform-specific policies. Pre-publication review is the cheapest risk mitigation available.

**How to apply:** When Jim or any agent drafts ad copy, run it through legal review before publishing. Check for: false claims, unsubstantiated claims, missing disclaimers, TCPA triggers, testimonial/endorsement compliance.
