### Weeks 2–4
- **Monday:** 1 educational post (rotate: cost factors, permit basics, materials, timeline, how to vet a builder).
- **Wednesday:** 1 photo post — real recent build (requires Ben or Garcia sharing photos; start asking for these in their next check-in).

### Ongoing golden rule
**2 helpful posts for every 1 that links to the website.** Nextdoor punishes promotional accounts hard. The algorithm rewards accounts that answer questions, not ones that sell.

### Template #2 — Educational (Day 3 / Weekly)

> Thinking about a new deck this summer? 3 things worth checking BEFORE you sign any contract with a Wisconsin deck builder:
>
> 1. **Are they licensed in the State of Wisconsin and insured for liability + worker's comp?** Ask for the certificate, not just a verbal "yes." If they hesitate, walk away.
>
> 2. **Are permits included in the quote, or extra?** Wausau permits a deck attached to the house; freestanding under 200 sq ft usually doesn't need one, but it varies by city. A legit builder knows this cold for your specific address.
>
> 3. **What's the payment schedule?** Anything more than a 10-25% deposit up front is a red flag in 2026. Most legit Central Wisconsin builders work milestone-based (design, framing, decking, final).
>
> Happy to answer deck questions in the thread — no pressure, just trying to be useful.
>
> More at cwdeckbuilders.com/faq if it helps.

## Part C — 3 Reply Templates (When Neighbors Ask)

Reply to ANY Nextdoor post mentioning "deck," "contractor recommendation," "backyard project," or "outdoor space" — **whether or not they mention us**.

### Reply #1 — Direct "who should I hire?" post

> Hi [name] — Central Wisconsin Deck Builders here. We work with licensed local builders across the Wausau area and send quotes within 48 hours. Happy to get you in touch with one of our builders if helpful — no pressure, no lead-selling.
>
> Either way, 2 quick filters to use on any builder you talk to: (a) ask for a WI license + COI, (b) ask if permits are priced in. Those two questions weed out 80% of the churn-and-burn shops.
>
> More at cwdeckbuilders.com if useful.

### Reply #2 — "What should I budget?" post

> Ballpark for Central Wisconsin deck work in 2026:
>
> - Pressure-treated, basic 12x16: ~$8k-$14k
> - Cedar, same size: ~$14k-$22k
> - Composite (Trex, TimberTech), same size: ~$20k-$32k
> - Multi-level or 20ft+ elevation: add 30-50%
>
> These are full-install numbers including framing, railings, stairs, permits for most WI municipalities. DIY material-only would be roughly 40% of those figures.
>
> We have a calculator at cwdeckbuilders.com/cost-calculator if you want to play with sizes. And happy to get you a real quote from a local builder in 48 hours if helpful.

### Reply #3 — "My deck is 20 years old, should I repair or replace?" post

> Rule of thumb: if the structure (joists, ledger, posts) is sound, repair/refinish is 30-50% of replacement cost and extends life 10-15 years. If the structure is rotting or moving, replace — you'll spend more patching it over the next 5 years than one clean rebuild.
>
> Quickest way to tell: poke a screwdriver into a few joists at the ledger. If it sinks in, the wood's gone.
>
> Happy to have one of our Central WI builders come out and give you an honest read — sometimes we tell people "your deck has another 10 years, don't spend the money." cwdeckbuilders.com

## Part D — What NOT to do

- **DO NOT post identical text to all 5 neighborhoods same day.** Nextdoor flags it as spam. Stagger 24-48 hrs between cross-posts. Vary opening lines per market.
- **DO NOT reply to every post with the same boilerplate.** Algorithm ranks based on originality. Reply uniquely or don't reply.
- **DO NOT use "click here" or link shorteners.** Full URLs only. Shortened URLs get filtered.
- **DO NOT post contractor photos without their written OK.** Ben and Garcia must consent before their jobsite photos go public on Nextdoor.
- **DO NOT respond defensively if someone complains or disputes us.** Acknowledge, apologize if warranted, offer to DM. Never argue publicly.
- **DO NOT post after 9 PM or before 7 AM.** Nextdoor engagement is 10 AM - 8 PM; off-hours posts get buried.

## Part F — Escalation Triggers (week 1)

Flag to CEO if:
- Any post gets flagged/removed by moderators → review content, adjust template, avoid re-posting.
- A neighbor accuses CWDB of being a lead-aggregator / shady / out-of-state → respond once, briefly, accurately; do not engage further publicly. DM the user to resolve privately.
- Any post gets >50 engagement (high for local feed) → screenshot, post to Outbox as a win, consider paid boost in week 3+.
- Any inbound lead from Nextdoor → log the source manually in HubSpot contact notes, and proof the organic channel is working.

## Rollback / Pause

If the business account gets a strike from Nextdoor moderation:
1. Pause all posting immediately.
2. Read the strike reason carefully — usually "promotional content in a neighbors-only feed."
3. Pivot to **reply-only** for 2 weeks (no new posts, only helpful replies to others).
4. Resume posting with more restrained language once account reputation stabilizes.
