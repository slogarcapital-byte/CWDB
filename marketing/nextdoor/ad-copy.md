## ORGANIC COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

### When to Use
Monitor Nextdoor neighborhood posts in Wausau, Schofield, Weston, Mosinee, and Merrill for:
- "Does anyone know a good deck builder?"
- "Can anyone recommend a deck contractor?"
- "Who built your deck? Looking for quotes"
- "Looking for a reliable contractor for a deck project"

### Organic Reply Template (conversational, not salesy)

**Version A (direct):**
> "Hey [Name]! We actually run a free deck quote service for Central Wisconsin homeowners — you fill out a short form and we connect you with a local licensed contractor within 24 hours. Totally free to request a quote: [URL]. Hope that helps!"

**Version B (neighbor tone):**
> "A few folks in the area have used [Brand Name] for this — it's a free service where you fill out what you're looking for and a local deck contractor reaches out. No obligation, just a quote. [URL]"

**Version C (social proof):**
> "We work with deck contractors across the Wausau area and this is the most common thing we hear from homeowners! Check out [URL] — free quote request, takes 2 minutes."

### Organic Reply Rules
- Always reply within the same day the post appears
- Never post in threads that already have a good contractor recommendation — don't crowd it
- One reply per post — don't spam the thread
- Be helpful first, promotional second
- Always include the landing page URL
- Track every reply in HubSpot or a simple spreadsheet (date, neighborhood, post type, link clicked)

## PERFORMANCE NOTES

- Nextdoor ads typically have lower CPL than Facebook/Instagram due to less advertiser competition
- Community/neighbor tone outperforms "ad-speak" on this platform
- Organic replies can generate leads at $0 cost — prioritize this in Phase 1
- Use UTM parameters on all Nextdoor links to track source in analytics: `?utm_source=nextdoor&utm_medium=organic` or `?utm_source=nextdoor&utm_medium=paid`
