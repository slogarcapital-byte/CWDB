# CWDB Ad Launch — Hook Matrix v2

## Intro

The avatar is the Stressed Delegator: a Central Wisconsin homeowner, 35-65, who wants a deck built or replaced but hates the contractor search. They have been ghosted, handed a napkin quote, or stood up on an estimate. They have a budget and a timeline. They are not shopping for inspiration. They want one form, one trusted name, one fast reply.

The offer is: fill one short form on `cwdeckbuilders.com/get-a-quote`, get matched with a licensed local deck builder in Wausau, Schofield, Weston, Mosinee, or Merrill, and get a real quote within 48 hours. No phone tag. No obligation. The click commitment is the form. The trust commitment is the 48-hour promise. The proof stack at launch is process proof (licensed, insured, local, 5-city coverage, 48-hour SLA) — not social proof. Social proof is honestly deferred until we bank the first 5 accepted bids and first reviews.

Wave 1 locked three shipping angles — Problem/Solution, Process Proof, Seasonal Urgency — and deferred Social Proof and Outcome until proof catches up. This matrix scores candidates across all six archetypes, picks the three winners, maps them to the locked ad placements, and holds two backup hooks in reserve for Week 3 rotation.

## Shipping Hooks — 3 Primary

### Hook 1 — Problem/Solution (ships to `m-as1-v1-problem` and `m-as2-v1-problem`)

**Winning phrase:** Tired of deck contractors who never call back?

**Why it wins:** The Stressed Delegator avatar's #1 fear is "chasing contractors who ghost you." This is their line, in their voice, before we sell anything. Reads at 4th-grade level. No forbidden words. Lever-3 honest — we are not claiming outcomes, we are naming a problem homeowners already live in.

**Full hook expansion (ad-ready):**

> Tired of deck contractors who never call back?
> Fill out one form. Get matched with a licensed local builder in Wausau, Schofield, Weston, Mosinee, or Merrill. Real quote in 48 hours.
> Get Quote.

Hammer voice. Short punches. Problem named in line 1, solution proved in line 2, CTA in line 3.

---

### Hook 2 — Process Proof (ships to `m-as1-v2-process` and Google AG1)

**Winning phrase:** Licensed. Insured. Local. Quote in 48 hours — not 48 days.

**Why it wins:** The Stressed Delegator's second fear is getting burned. Process proof is what earns trust when we have no social proof to lean on. "48 hours not 48 days" is the specificity hook — it turns a claim into a contrast. All true, all defensible, no puffery.

**Full hook expansion (ad-ready):**

> Licensed. Insured. Local.
> Get a real deck quote from a Central Wisconsin builder in 48 hours — not 48 days.
> One short form. No phone tag. No obligation.
> Get Quote.

Hammer voice with a specificity twist. Every line adds one concrete fact. "No phone tag" is the avatar's pain point, said their way.

---

### Hook 3 — Seasonal Urgency (ships to `m-as2-v2-urgency`)

**Winning phrase:** Wisconsin summer is short. Good builders book up by May.

**Why it wins:** Honest scarcity — Wisconsin's build season really is May to October, and the good crews really do fill their calendars by late spring. No manufactured "act now" pressure. The avatar already knows this; the ad just surfaces the deadline they're half-aware of.

**Full hook expansion (ad-ready):**

> Wisconsin summer is short. The good deck builders book up by May.
> Fill out one form. A vetted local builder sends a real quote in 48 hours.
> Lock your spot for summer 2026.
> Get Quote.

Hammer voice, seasonal-truth framing. "Lock your spot" is action language without being salesy because the underlying scarcity is real.
