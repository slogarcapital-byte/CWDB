## Usage Notes

All images below are sourced from Unsplash, Pexels, or Pixabay.

- **Unsplash License** — Free for commercial use, no attribution required. Download at full resolution from the photo page.
- **Pexels License** — Free for commercial use, no attribution required. Download at full resolution from the photo page.
- **Pixabay License** — Free for commercial use, no attribution required (CC0).

All Unsplash images should be downloaded at maximum resolution (typically 4000–6000px wide). Pexels and Pixabay offer "Free Download" buttons with size options — always select the largest available.

Target minimum: 1440px wide. Recommended: 2400px+ for retina displays.

## Download Instructions

1. Open the photo URL in a browser.
2. Click "Download free" (Unsplash) or "Free Download" (Pexels).
3. Select the largest available size — typically labeled "Original" or the highest pixel count.
4. Rename the file to match the city slug before uploading to [[Webflow]] (e.g., `wausau-hero.jpg`, `merrill-hero.jpg`).
5. In [[Webflow]], upload to the Asset Manager before referencing in CMS or page sections.

## What to Avoid

- Palm trees, tropical plants, tile patios — these read as Southern/Southwest and will undermine local credibility.
- Resort or hotel-style pool decks — too aspirational and inconsistent with Central Wisconsin homeowner expectations.
- Generic blue-sky stock photos with no architectural context — no emotional connection for the homeowner.
- Images that appear AI-generated or overly polished — authenticity matters in a trust-based contractor market.
