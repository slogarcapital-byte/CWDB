# SBG Construction Group (under evaluation)

Jim is evaluating going full-time into construction with Ben Barton (Barton Builders LLC) and John Garcia (John Garcia Construction LLC) as "SBG Construction Group" (Slogar, Barton, Garcia). This extends the [[fulfillment-model-pivot-2026-06-10]] and, if pursued, shifts CWDB from a lead-gen experiment toward a construction operating business.

## Decided structure (partner meeting 2026-06-17, refined via interview 2026-06-18)
Shared-services / captive-labor group, NOT a merger. Two phases:
- **Phase A (now, 1 to 2 yrs):** the three existing LLCs stay independent customer-facing contractors, each signs its own jobs and KEEPS ITS OWN JOB PROFIT. SBG owns shared crews + equipment + (later) real estate and bills the LLCs at market rates.
- **Phase B (1 to 2 yrs out):** transition to a true merger where job profit pools and splits 1/3. Trigger + valuation/equalization of the three unequal LLCs + (generic) pre-existing-asset re-pricing decided NOW in the term sheet, not at the transition.

## Entities
Three sibling SBG entities, owned 1/3 each by the three INDIVIDUALS (flag for attorney), funded by EQUAL CASH (~$30k to $45k each, to be sized against equipment list + payroll float):
- **SBG-Labor = S-CORP** (a partner cannot be a W-2 employee of a partnership, so S-corp is required for the partners to draw real W-2 wages). Employs the crews + the three partners.
- **SBG-Equipment** = partnership LLC; buys its own equipment with the equal cash; leases to the LLCs.
- **SBG-RealEstate** = partnership LLC, DORMANT for now (no facility purchase yet; partners use own space).

## Pay / money (Phase A)
Partner income = own-LLC job profit (siloed, whoever books more keeps more) + an **$80/hr W-2 market wage** from SBG for hours worked. SBG bills at market and REINVESTS its profit (no 1/3 distribution yet).
- **Derived billable rate from the $80 wage: ~$145/hr WITH workers' comp, ~$135/hr if partners elect out of WC.** Build-up: $80 wage + employer FICA 7.65% + FUTA/WI SUTA + WC (carpentry code 5645, 8.53% verified) + ~$12 overhead, then the ~85% billable-utilization step ($105.16 paid-hr cost / 0.85 = $123.72 per billable hr) + ~17% margin = ~$145. SBG-Labor margin ~$21/billable hr (~$64k / $106k / $149k a yr at 3k / 5k / 7k billable hrs; an earlier draft overstated this ~2x by mixing paid-hour cost with billable-hour billing, now fixed). Pay-policy choice: pay all clocked hours (~$145) vs billable-only (~$126).
