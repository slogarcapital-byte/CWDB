# Decision: Pay Per Accepted Bid at $1,000

## Context
First contractor commitment secured. Needed to set the pricing model for how contractors pay for leads.

## Options Considered
1. **Pay per lead ($80-$150)** — contractor pays for every lead regardless of outcome. Standard in industry (Angi, Thumbtack model).
2. **Territory license ($1,200/mo)** — flat monthly fee for exclusive access to leads in a territory.
3. **Pay per accepted bid ($1,000)** — contractor only pays when they win a job from our lead.

## Decision
**Pay per accepted bid at $1,000.** Contractor pays when the homeowner accepts their bid on a CWDB-sourced lead.

**Why this model:**
- Eliminates contractor risk — they only pay when they earn revenue
- Higher per-unit revenue ($1,000 vs $80-$150) compensates for lower conversion rate
- Builds contractor trust — we're incentivized to send quality leads
- 5-layer verification system ensures honest reporting

## Impact
- Revenue per accepted bid: $1,000
- Target cost per accepted bid: <$400 (at 20% close rate, 5 leads × $60 CPL)
- Target margin: ~$700 per accepted bid
- All contractor agreements, financial projections, and agent configs use this model
