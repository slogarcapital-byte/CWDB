# Pricing Model

**Why:** Core revenue mechanism. All optimization flows from understanding unit economics.

**How to apply:** Use as baseline for all revenue projections, ad budget decisions, and pricing experiments.

## Primary Model: Pay Per Accepted Bid
- Contractor pays $1,000 when homeowner accepts their bid on a CWDB-sourced lead
- Decision: [[2026-03-12 Pay Per Accepted Bid Pricing]]

## Unit Economics (Projected)
```
Ad spend per lead:           $20–$60
Leads per accepted bid:      ~5 (at 20% close rate)
Cost per accepted bid:       $100–$300
Revenue per accepted bid:    $1,000
Margin per accepted bid:     $700–$900
```

## Monthly Projections (at scale)
| Scenario | Leads/mo | Accepted Bids | Revenue | Ad Spend | Margin |
|----------|----------|--------------|---------|----------|--------|
| Conservative | 20 | 4 | $4,000 | $1,200 | $2,800 |
| Target | 35 | 7 | $7,000 | $2,100 | $4,900 |
| Aggressive | 50 | 10 | $10,000 | $3,000 | $7,000 |

## Future Pricing Options
- Territory licensing: ~$1,200/mo per city (flat fee)
- Multi-contractor marketplace: multiple contractors bid on same lead (higher rev/lead)
- Tiered pricing: premium placement = higher fee
