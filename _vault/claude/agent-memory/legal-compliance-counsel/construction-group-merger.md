## What the deal is NOW: "SBG Construction Group" (Slogar, Barton, Garcia)

A **shared-services / captive-labor** structure, NOT a merger. The three existing LLCs (Jim's Central Wisconsin Deck Builders LLC, Ben's Barton Builders LLC, John's John Garcia Construction LLC) STAY independent and customer-facing. Each signs its own jobs, pulls its own permits under its OWN Dwelling Contractor Certification + Qualifier, carries its OWN GL, and KEEPS ITS OWN JOB PROFIT. SBG is a back-office group the three LLCs buy labor and equipment from.

**Three new SBG entities, owned 1/3 each by the three INDIVIDUALS (not the LLCs), funded by EQUAL CASH:**
- **SBG-Labor** = employs the W-2 crews AND the 3 partners; leases labor to the LLCs. Accounting recommends S-corp tax treatment so partners are true W-2 employees. SBG-Labor carries the workers' comp.
- **SBG-Equipment** = owns equipment (bought w/ equal cash); leases it to the LLCs. Needs WI DOR sales-tax registration for the rentals.
- **SBG-RealEstate** = formed but DORMANT (no facility purchase yet).

**Key decided positions (defend for consistency):**
- Capital = EQUAL CASH only. NO deemed-equal equipment/backlog/brand. Equipment a partner wants in = arm's-length FMV SALE, not a contribution.
- SBG bills the LLCs at MARKET rates and REINVESTS its Phase A profit (no 1/3 distribution yet; mandatory tax distributions still required).
- Jim's lead engine stays 100% CWDB's, feeds only CWDB jobs. NOT licensed/contributed to SBG. The old $1,000-per-accepted-bid lead deal w/ Ben/John is being WOUND DOWN (clean final accounting + written termination).
- Each partner is a W-2 employee of SBG-Labor for hours worked.
- Ownership at the INDIVIDUAL level is a FLAG FOR ATTORNEY (no LLC buffer; personal creditors/divorce/estate reach the SBG stake directly). Buy-sell drafted to cover whichever ownership form the attorney picks.

## Licensing split (the structure's advantage)
SBG does NOT contract with homeowners, so the heavy dwelling-contractor gate sits on the 3 operating LLCs (each its own prime, each its own cert+qualifier+GL; credentials do NOT transfer between entities; Jim's LLC still gated until Jim is certified). SBG's narrower gates: SBG-Labor needs WC (Wis. Stat. ch. 102) + payroll + WI withholding/UI + S-corp election; SBG-Equipment needs WI DOR sales-tax registration for rentals. **SBG likely needs NO Dwelling Contractor Certification as a lessor: CONFIRM with WI DSPS, do not assume.**

## Trickiest legal interaction (track it)
**Leased-labor / WC-exemption interaction:** each LLC's Dwelling Contractor Certification requires WC-coverage-OR-exemption. When an LLC uses LEASED crews from SBG-Labor (not its own employees), work out with insurer + WI DSPS how that LLC satisfies the requirement: LLC relies on its own solo-owner exemption WHILE leased workers are covered under SBG-Labor's comp + the special-employer/borrowed-servant doctrine. Confirm DSPS accepts this on each cert. This is the hinge of Inter-Company Agreement #3.

3. Confirm current WI elements w/ attorney.
